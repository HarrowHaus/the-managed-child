§1The sequence

In 1885 she joined the Fabian Society in its second year, when it had fewer than forty members.1 She contributed to Fabian Essays; Shaw called her "the greatest orator in England."1Dates checked against the Fabians' own materials and Besant's Autobiography. The two phases were sequential, not simultaneous. documented.

Then in 1889 she converted to Theosophy, and let her Fabian membership lapse. She became President of the Theosophical Society (1907–1933) — architect of the Order of the Star in the East, the project that groomed Krishnamurti as a "vehicle" for the World‑Teacher.22Mary Lutyens, The Years of Awakening — the authorized biography is itself the managed-child thesis. documented.

§2What keeps it honest

PrecisionThe honest claim is not "a Fabian plot ran the Theosophical Society." It is the sturdier, stranger fact: one of the era's foremost managerial-socialist organizers carried the same disposition — society improved by a guiding, initiated elite shaping the many, and especially the young — from the secular idiom straight into the esoteric one.
