# anti-patterns.md — worked before/after (critic calibration; the "before" must score REVISE)

### 1. Throat-clearing opener → flat load-bearing opener
- BEFORE: "In an age defined by the relentless march of institutions, one cannot help but ask: who truly shapes the child?"
- AFTER: "Most of the people who built the managed child had no occult connection at all."

### 2. Announced takeaway → let the fact land
- BEFORE: "This is deeply significant, and it underscores the crucial fact that the primary text itself makes the connection clear for us."
- AFTER: "A sovereign child and a few who rule the many, welded inside one document."

### 3. Uniform rhythm → burstiness
- BEFORE: "Watson conditioned children. He believed in detachment. His book explained the method. It became influential over time."
- AFTER: "Watson's ideal child is one who 'has no great attachments to any place or person.' Engineered detachment, named by the architect as a design goal, in his own book. He is more damning unparaphrased than any critic could make him."

### 4. Inflated diction / lexical tells → plain muscular prose
- BEFORE: "Bacon's rich tapestry of ideas would go on to profoundly resonate, illuminating a robust and multifaceted vision that fostered the scientific realm."
- AFTER: "The Royal Society took the fable as an ideal. The robes became lab coats; the structure held."

### 5. Both-sides mush → adjudicated with the seam marked
- BEFORE: "While some might argue the schools were designed as obedience factories, others disagree, and the truth is nuanced."
- AFTER: "Cubberley's sentence is a documented sentence. It is not, by itself, proof that American schooling was designed as an obedience factory — that sweeping story is contested and is not asserted. The narrower claim survives."
