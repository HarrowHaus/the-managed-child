## THE SHORT ANSWER
What we have built is not an essay and not a book. It is a **knowledge graph**: nodes (figures, works, institutions), edges (welds and hypotheses, each graded), and a thesis the graph supports. That structure is *why* the question has more than one answer — a graph can be **read** as an argument (a book), **elaborated** as essays about the work (thesis/lineage/method), and **browsed** as a reference (a database/site). All three draw from the same spine. You don't have to choose one. You have to choose the **order**, and guard against the one real trap.

## THE FOUR TIERS (they nest; each feeds the next)

### Tier 0 — THE DATABASE / KNOWLEDGE BASE  *(the infrastructure — your "encyclopedic" instinct, correctly placed)*
Every figure, work, institution, weld, and date as a **structured, cross-linked entry carrying its grade.** This is the thing you intuited: expand every thread into an entry, link the entries, and you have an encyclopedia of the lineage.
- **What makes it novel (and defensible):** it is the **anti-conspiracy wiki** — every edge is *rated* ([WELD] / [HYPOTHESIS] / [FICTION-ALERT]), every discard is *named*, every antisemitic or cabal claim is handled by the hard sourcing rule *on the page*. No other treatment of this material shows its work. The grading, made browsable, is a form nobody else in the space has.
- **Its role: infrastructure, not product.** It is the single source of truth the book and the essays both draw from. Build a claim once, grade it once, cite it everywhere.
- **Form:** a linked set of Markdown entries now (what we already have, atomized), later a static site or a proper wiki/graph database.

## THE ONE TRAP (name it so we don't fall in)
**"Encyclopedic" is both the opportunity and the danger.** The graph *can* expand forever — every figure has a teacher, every teacher a school, every school a city. But:
- **Encyclopedism is the enemy of argument.** An encyclopedia has entries, not a thesis. The thing that made this work strong is *selection and grading* — the discipline of the audit. "Expand every thread" is infinite, and infinity never ships.
- **The encyclopedic urge can be sophisticated procrastination** — a way to keep researching so as never to finish arguing. The completeness feels like progress while the book that would actually land the thesis never gets written.
- **The rule:** the database serves the products; it does not replace them. Expand a thread when the **book or an essay needs it**, or when a node is **load-bearing and still only [HYPOTHESIS].** Do not expand threads for completeness. Let the argument pull the research, not the reverse.

## RECOMMENDED SEQUENCE
1. **Atomize the bank into the database (Tier 0) — the right next build.** Convert the three working files into linked, graded entries. This is your encyclopedic instinct, executed as *infrastructure*. It loses nothing and makes everything downstream faster. It is also the natural home for the "every detail" impulse — safely, because entries are graded, not sprawling.
2. **Write the book (Tier 1) as the flagship** — traverse the graph with the thesis. This is where the strongest, most defensible work lives, and it's what the whole audit was quietly preparing.
3. **Write essays about the work (Tier 2) in parallel** — essays on the thesis/lineage/method, each grounded in `data/nodes/**` and linking claims to their nodes. They can ship while the book is written; they feed interest back to it. *(The media decodes are parked — DECISIONS.md S-12 — and reintroduced later.)*
4. **Stand up the website (Tier 3) last** — once there's a book spine and a few essays, the container that cross-links reference + argument + essays becomes worth building.

**One decision to make now, before any building:** *is the flagship the book or the site?* If the ambition is a durable intellectual work, the book leads and the site serves it. If the ambition is a living, growing public reference, the site (Tier 0 made public) leads and the book is extracted from it later. Both are legitimate; they imply different next moves. Everything else in the sequence is stable regardless of that choice.
