# Double-parentage (liberation + control)

The freeing that hands you over. To take the child from the family, in the name of raising him higher, is already to place him under someone else.

## The frame
The pattern: a child is detached from ordinary family life — presented as rescue, elevation, or liberation — and the detachment is what makes expert administration possible. Robert Nisbet gave the general argument: loosening a person from the intermediate ties of family and locality does not produce a free individual but a more manageable one, delivered to a larger authority. The `krishnamurti-custody` case is the version that went to court; Plato dissolving the guardians' families and Watson's engineered detachment of the infant are the same move in other rooms.

## Held-open / discard
This is a lens, not a documented mechanism. Nisbet's thesis is a cited argument, not a fact this study proves, and it is used to *read* the documented cases, never as evidence in its own right. Where a case is real — the custody suit, Watson's own text — the case carries the weight; the frame only lines them up.

## Role in the thesis
The reading applied to "liberation" wherever the material offers it: ask what administration the freeing enables. A lens on the documented record, not a claim of hidden design.
