# The Babalon Working

> **WELD** · map node 4.1 · 1946

A documented ritual series, and the exact line between what was done and what was claimed to have happened.

## Documented connection
Between **January and March 1946**, at the Parsonage in Pasadena, **Jack Parsons** conducted a series of rituals with **L. Ron Hubbard** as scribe and seer, drawing on Crowley's sex magick and the "**moonchild**" concept from Crowley's 1917 novel. Parsons's own **Book of Babalon** (Liber 49) is the primary record; the received text declares, "**Yea, it is I, BABALON. And this is my book, that is the fourth chapter of the Book of the Law**," and Parsons declared himself the Antichrist "**Belarion**." Crowley, watching by correspondence from London, was contemptuous — he wrote to Karl Germer that he was "**fairly frantic when I contemplate the idiocy of these louts!**"

## Asserted intent — firewalled
Whether the working *did* anything — summoned Babalon, produced consequences — is **Parsons's own belief**, recorded in his own text, not a documented fact. Parsons read the arrival of the artist **Marjorie Cameron** as the elemental the ritual had called; that reading is his interpretation. The event is documented; its efficacy is a claim its own author makes.

## Held-open
- The reception date of Liber 49 is given as February 27, 28, and 29 across sources; all recorded, none picked.
- Verses 5–8 of Liber 49 are "missing and presumed lost."

## Role in the thesis
The rail's clearest specimen of the connection/intent split held inside a single event: the ritual is documented to the letter from its author's own hand, while everything it claims to have *accomplished* stays exactly where the evidence leaves it — an assertion, not a result.
