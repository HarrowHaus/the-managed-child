# The Ojai Convergence

> **WELD** · map node 3.3 · 1922-1946

One California valley where the century's child-project ran its whole arc — raised to a planned destiny, repudiated, then rebuilt as its opposite.

## Documented core
Ojai is where several strands of the managed-child story sit in one place, and the convergence is documented person by person. The Theosophists brought **Krishnamurti** to Ojai's **Arya Vihara** from 1922, grooming him as the vehicle of the coming **World Teacher** under **Annie Besant** and the **Order of the Star in the East**. In 1929, at Ommen, Krishnamurti **dissolved that order himself** — "**Truth is a pathless land**" — repudiating the role a movement had raised him for. He remained at Ojai the rest of his life.

Into that milieu came **Aldous Huxley**, who settled in Southern California in 1937 and became close to Krishnamurti and to Gerald Heard. In **1946** Huxley and Krishnamurti — with Rosalind Rajagopal and Guido Ferrando — founded the **Happy Valley School** (now Besant Hill School of Happy Valley) on land Besant's Theosophical foundation had purchased in 1927: a deliberately **anti-conditioning, child-led** school, the near-opposite of the World-Teacher grooming that had first drawn the movement to the valley. The same town, within one lifetime, held both the child administered toward a planned end and the child freed to its own will.

## Held-open / discard
This is a **convergence of documented lives in one place, not a coordinated program**. An earlier placeholder shorthand, "Campbell circle," is dropped: no documented Joseph Campbell tie to the Ojai milieu was located, and none is asserted. The reading that the valley was an *engineered* convergence — a single hand arranging the World-Teacher project, its collapse, and the school — has no documentary basis and is not drawn; what is documented is that the same disposition (the child as material to be brought to its destined form) surfaced here in both its administering and its liberating idioms.

## Role in the thesis
The lifespan rail (map-node 3.3): a place where the double-parentage of the managed child is legible in miniature — grooming toward a planned destiny, its public repudiation, and a liberation-education experiment, all in one valley across one generation. Cited as itself, a documented milieu — never generalized into a design (S-5).
