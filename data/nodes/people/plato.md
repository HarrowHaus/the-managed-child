# Plato

> **WELD** · map node Cap.1a · c. 428–348 BCE

The source text of the trunk. Rule by a trained guardian class, secured by a story taught to the young, is set down plainly two and a half thousand years before there was a profession to carry it out.

## Documented core [WELD]
In the *Republic* Plato orders the city by a trained **guardian class** and equips it with a founding falsehood — the **noble lie** of Book III (414b–415c), the myth of the metals, by which citizens are told they were formed in the earth with gold, silver, or bronze in their souls, so that each accepts the station he is held to fit. Plato is explicit that the story is **taught from childhood**. In Book V he goes further and dissolves the guardians' own families, their children raised in common and parentage deliberately obscured, so that the city may form the young directly rather than compete with the household for them.

## Role in the thesis
The trunk's origin, cited as itself and never generalised into a plot: the doctrine that a knowing few should shape the many — if need be through myth, and especially through the child — stated at the head of Western political thought, and recurring after it by publication rather than by any relay.
