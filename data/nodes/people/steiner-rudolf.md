# Rudolf Steiner

> **WELD** · map node 1.5 · 1861-1925

The second great schismatic — out of theosophy over the enthroned child, and into an esoteric pedagogy of his own.

## Documented connection
**Steiner** led the **German Section** of the Theosophical Society as its General Secretary, then broke with it: he **founded the Anthroposophical Society on 28 December 1912** and was **expelled from the TS on 7 March 1913**. The break turned on the Adyar leadership's identification of **Jiddu Krishnamurti** as the vehicle of the coming World Teacher (the Order of the Star), which Steiner rejected, along with the dilution of Christian elements; roughly **fifty-five of sixty-nine German lodges left with him**. His Anthroposophy went on to produce the **Waldorf** schools — an esoteric account of child development in an educational idiom.

## Asserted intent — firewalled
That theosophy (or Anthroposophy) **produced a managed-child program** through Waldorf pedagogy is not a claim the record supports; the documented facts are the schism and Steiner's own doctrine. The Waldorf tie is noted as an adjacency into the education rail, not asserted as a directed instrument.

## Role in the thesis
Two things at once: another break that disproves a single directed lineage (theosophy fissions again, this time over the world-teacher child), and a second esoteric route toward the enactment rail — the child as the thing an initiated doctrine organizes itself around, here in schooling. The recurrence is documented; the coordination is what keeps splitting apart.
