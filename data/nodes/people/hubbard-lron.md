# L. Ron Hubbard

> **WELD** · map node 4.1 · 1911-1986

The scribe of the Babalon Working who founded a religion — and the exact place the documented connection and the contested derivation must be held apart.

## Documented connection
In **August 1945** Hubbard moved into Jack Parsons's Pasadena house; the two formed **Allied Enterprises**, and Hubbard served as scribe for the **1946 Babalon Working**. The venture ended with Hubbard and Sara Northrup leaving with Parsons's money and boats — Crowley's contemporaneous verdict, by letter, was "**Suspect Ron playing confidence trick**." That Hubbard **knew Crowley's work** is documented in his own voice: on the 1952 Philadelphia Doctorate Course tape he calls Crowley "**the late Aleister Crowley, my very good friend**" and recommends Crowley's *Master Therion* as "the only modern work that has anything to do with" the magic cults. He and Crowley never met in person.

## Asserted intent — attributed and contested
That **Scientology derives from Crowley** is a separate, weaker claim, and it belongs to named claimants. **Jon Atack** argues Crowley's magick "is at the core of Scientology." The scholar **Hugh Urban** holds it to a strict ceiling: Crowley's ideas are "one—but only one—element in the rich, eclectic bricolage" of early Scientology, mixed with Eastern religion, science fiction, and pop psychology. **J. Gordon Melton** rejects the connection as core. The documented fact is that Hubbard read and praised Crowley; the derivation of a religion from that reading is argued, not established, and it is not treated as documented here.

## Held-open
- The sum Hubbard took from Parsons is given as $10,000, "over $20,000," and "nearly $21,000" across sources. All recorded; none picked.
- The Church of Scientology's counter-narrative — that Hubbard "was working as an undercover policeman for the LAPD when he infiltrated a black-magic ring" — is the Church's own attributed, uncorroborated claim, logged as such.
- Ronald DeWolf (Hubbard's estranged son) made stronger claims in a 1983 *Penthouse* interview; a hostile witness who retracted other allegations. Flagged, not relied on.

## Role in the thesis
The hinge where a documented biographical connection (Parsonage, scribe, read Crowley) is most often laundered into an unproven doctrinal derivation. Holding the two apart — read, documented; derived, contested — is the method working on its hardest case.
