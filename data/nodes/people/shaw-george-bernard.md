# George Bernard Shaw

> **WELD** · map node 0.2 · 1856-1950

The doctrine's most articulate daylight voice — the founding Fabian who fixed gradualism as public creed, boasted the method from the podium, and printed the breeding-the-stock idiom in a West End comedy's appendix.

## Documented core
Shaw joined the **Fabian Society in 1884**, sat on its **Executive Committee from 1885 to 1911**, wrote the Society's early **Manifesto** (Fabian Tract No. 2, 1884), and became its foremost pamphleteer. He **edited *Fabian Essays in Socialism*** (Fabian Society, December 1889), wrote its Preface, and authored two of its eight essays ("Economic"; "Transition"). This — the daylight articulation of Fabian gradualism, signed and public — is the node's work near the origin of the map.

Three years later, in **Fabian Tract No. 41, *The Fabian Society: What It Has Done and How It Has Done It*** (read at Essex Hall, 6 February 1892), Shaw named the method and boasted of it: **"We permeated the party organizations and pulled all the wires we could lay our hands on with our utmost adroitness and energy."** The *word* "permeation" and the wire-pulling boast belong to **1892**, not to the 1889 *Essays* — the volume states gradualism as doctrine; the strategy-by-name is here.

Shaw's superman theme runs the same channel. In *Man and Superman* (1903) and its systematic sequel *Back to Methuselah* (1921), Creative Evolution — the Life Force, not Darwinian selection — drives humanity toward the Superman: **"We must replace the man by the superman."** It is the managed-improvement idiom in his own hand.

## Held-open / discard
Shaw's **eugenics is real, in his own printed words, and it is 1903 — not 1889.** In "The Revolutionist's Handbook," the appendix to *Man and Superman* (stated in-character as the play's John Tanner, a device Shaw owned), he wrote: **"The only fundamental and possible Socialism is the socialization of the selective breeding of Man: in other terms, of human evolution."** That printed line carries the documented eugenics weight. The much-quoted **"lethal chamber"** rhetoric — "a great many people would have to be put out of existence simply because it wastes other people's time to look after them" — is a **press report** of a spoken 1910 lecture to the Eugenics Education Society (*Daily Express*, 4 March 1910), and the 1934 "humane gas … deadly by all means, but humane" is a BBC broadcast (*The Listener*): both are attributed reports of remarks in Shaw's deliberate shock-provocation register, weaker in standing than the printed line, reported at strength and never laundered. **None of this attaches to *Fabian Essays*** (R-3) — it is Shaw the man, and the advocacy face of the eugenics cluster, not the 1889 volume.

The 1930s **dictatorship praise** is the firewall stratum: after his 1931 USSR visit and three-hour meeting with Stalin, Shaw called Stalin a **"giant"** among Western "pigmies" and said **"I have seen the 'terrors' and I was terribly pleased by them."** Documented, attributed, dated — the same trained-elite disposition curdled into strong-man worship in old age; a late symptom, not the node's center. ## Role in the thesis
The Administrators rail, near the origin (map-node 0.2): the single most articulate daylight voice fusing the trained-elite/gradualist doctrine with the eugenic register — society improved by a fit, guiding minority, the stock bred toward its fit function. Its force is that Shaw *boasted* it, openly, in signed tracts and printed plays, which is exactly why the doctrine's recurrence needs no hidden cabal. Articulation, not cause (S-5): no line runs from Shaw into any child-institution, and the managed-child tie is the analyst's shared-root resemblance, never a chain.
