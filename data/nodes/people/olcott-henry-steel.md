# Henry Steel Olcott

> **WELD** · map node 1.5 · 1832-1907

The Society's first president and its most worldly founder — a lawyer and army officer who built the institution while Blavatsky built the doctrine.

## Documented connection
Colonel **Henry Steel Olcott**, an American officer, lawyer, and journalist, co-founded the **Theosophical Society** in 1875 and served as its **first President** until his death in 1907. He moved the headquarters to **Adyar**, where he founded the **Adyar Library and Research Centre**. In Ceylon he formally became a **Buddhist**, worked to revive Buddhism, and wrote the ***Buddhist Catechism*** (1881), still in use; the Society founded Buddhist schools — Ananda College (1886) among them — and he is honored in Sri Lanka to this day.

## Role in the thesis
The administrative half of the founding: where Blavatsky supplies the esoteric doctrine (the Masters, the coming evolution), Olcott supplies the organization — the charter, the presidency, the library, the schools. He is the reminder that the esoteric idiom, like the others in this study, ran on committees and buildings as much as on visions; the institution outlived the séance.
