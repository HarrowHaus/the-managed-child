# C. G. Jung

> **WELD** · map node 1.2 · 1875-1961

The substrate — the psychologist whose archetypes and collective unconscious became the shared idiom the twentieth-century managed culture drew on, carried into American elite culture by the Mellons' Bollingen machine.

## Documented core
Across a dated corpus — *Psychological Types* (1921), the widely-read *Modern Man in Search of a Soul* (1933), *The Psychology of the Child Archetype* with Kerényi (1940), and the essays gathered as *The Archetypes and the Collective Unconscious* (CW 9i) — Jung stated the load-bearing claim: beneath the personal unconscious lies a deeper layer that is **"not individual but universal… the same everywhere and in all individuals,"** made of **archetypes**, "a second psychic system of a collective, universal, and impersonal nature." His own guard keeps the claim precise: **"the representations themselves are not inherited, only the forms"** — inherited *structuring form*, not inherited images. The 1940 essay ties him directly to the **divine-child** archetype.

The substrate reached the mid-century managerial-cultural elite through two documented relays. **Eranos** — the annual conference at Ascona founded in 1933 by Olga Fröbe-Kapteyn, with Jung its gravitational center and a circle running to Kerényi, Scholem, Corbin, Eliade, and later Campbell — is the milieu where depth-psychology fused with comparative religion. **Bollingen** — the series inaugurated 1943 and the foundation established 1945, **named after Jung's tower on Lake Zurich** and conceived by Paul and Mary Mellon expressly to publish his Collected Works in English — is the channel that carried Jungian myth into American publishing, the same imprint that published Campbell's *Hero with a Thousand Faces* (Series XVII) and the Eranos papers (Series XXX). Campbell demonstrably worked off Jung: he edited *The Portable Jung* (1971) and those Eranos volumes, and built the monomyth on the archetypes.

## Held-open / discard
Two charged strands are kept peripheral and split. The **Nazi-era** material is documented and dated as *acts* — Jung accepted the presidency of the (reorganized) General Medical Society for Psychotherapy in June 1933 and edited its *Zentralblatt* until 1939/40, and a December 1933 editorial distinguished "Aryan" from "Jewish" psychology — while the *character* claim of antisemitism or collaboration is attributed and genuinely contested (his defenders note he restructured the society so barred German-Jewish practitioners could join as individuals; his authorization of the editorial is disputed). The acts happened; the intent is not settled; neither disturbs the documented standing of the archetype corpus. The **occult "Jung-as-magus"** reading — resting on the *Red Book* (composed 1914–1930, published 2009), Philemon, and his alchemy studies — marks a real esoteric strand, but the reading of Jung as a conscious occult operator is interpretive, not documented, and off-center. And the tie to Crowley's Aeon of the Child is **parallel emergence, not lineage** — no transmission runs between them.

## Role in the thesis
The esoteric/dispersal substrate (map-node 1.2): Jung is at once a *parallel* articulator of the enthroned child (the divine-child archetype, cognate to `aeon-of-the-child` with no line between them) and the *substrate* that fed the pop-dispersal through Campbell and the scholarly prestige-network through Eranos and Bollingen. Evidence of the culture that wanted a universal, expert-legible map of the inner life — a **symptom, not a cause** (S-5). The arrow from the archetypes to the managed child is the reader's analogy, left un-drawn as history; no line runs from Jung into any child-institution, and no design is imputed.
