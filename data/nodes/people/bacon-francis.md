# Francis Bacon

> **WELD** · map node Cap.1c · 1561–1626

The trunk in a lab coat. Bacon takes Plato's knowing few and re-dresses them as a fraternity of scientists who decide what the public may know.

## Documented core [WELD]
In *New Atlantis* (1627) the island of Bensalem is administered by **Salomon's House**, a fraternity of philosopher-scientists. Its head describes their practice in words worth quoting exactly: "we take all an oath of secrecy for the concealing of those which we think fit to keep secret." The elect decides what the public may know. The **Royal Society**, founded a generation later, took the fable as a working ideal; the robes became lab coats and the structure held.

## Role in the thesis
The bridge from the ancient guardian doctrine to its modern, scientific-managerial form — cited as a published text, never as a hidden hand. That later institutions read Bacon and adopted his ideal is documented influence, and it is the ordinary way a doctrine travels.
