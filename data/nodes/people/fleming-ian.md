# Ian Fleming

> **WELD** · map node DP.3 · 1908-1964

The naval-intelligence officer who invented James Bond — a documented spymaster's aide whose one famous occult tie turns out to be a biographer's anecdote.

## Documented core
Ian Fleming was recruited in May 1939 by the Director of Naval Intelligence, Rear Admiral John Godfrey, as his personal assistant — codename **17F**, working out of **Room 39** at the Admiralty. He rose from RNVR lieutenant to lieutenant-commander and sat close to the whole run of wartime naval deception: the **"Trout Memo"** (September 1939, issued under Godfrey's name but reflecting Fleming's thinking) whose item 28 seeded **Operation Mincemeat** (1943), and the commando **30 Assault Unit** (1942) he helped conceive. After the war he turned that world into fiction: ***Casino Royale*** (written at Goldeneye in early 1952, published 1953) launched the **James Bond** canon — twelve novels through 1966 that industrialized the secret-agent myth as global mass entertainment. That is the node's documented core, and it is solid.

## Held-open / discard
The node's famous "occult" tie is the R-8 test, and it does **not** survive as documented. The story that Fleming, with Maxwell Knight, proposed using **Aleister Crowley** to interrogate **Rudolf Hess** after Hess's May 1941 flight to Britain — and that Crowley wrote to the NID offering his services — rests **almost entirely on John Pearson's 1966 biography**, relating Fleming's own hedged anecdote; it appears nowhere in the independent archival record (not even on Fleming's Wikipedia page), and the Cavendish-Hotel lunches said to connect the two are an unverified recollection. It is graded **apocryphal / attributed, held behind Pearson**, not stated as fact. The wider "occultists won the war" and "Bond as predictive programming" readings are likewise firewalled behind their claimants (Spence's contested *Secret Agent 666*; McNeff's explicit novel) — no occult cabal is routed through the intelligence milieu.

Honesty about payload: Fleming's thesis-relevance is **modest**. What is real is the documented intelligence career, the shared deception milieu with **Dennis Wheatley**, and the pop-culture dispersal of the secret-agent myth — a DP.3 sibling to the monomyth's DP.1.

## Role in the thesis
The dispersal register (map-node DP.3): the administered world of intelligence handlers rendered as mass entertainment — a symptom of the century's appetite for the secret-agent myth, not a cause of anything (S-5). The occult legend that drew the node into the corpus is, on inspection, a single biographer's anecdote, and is held there as exactly that.
