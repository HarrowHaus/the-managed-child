# COINTELPRO

> **WELD** · map node Audit.5 · 1956-1971

A covert program of domestic political disruption, documented in the FBI's own words and condemned in the congressional record.

## Documented connection
From **1956 to 1971**, under Director **J. Edgar Hoover**, the FBI ran **COINTELPRO** — covert operations to surveil, infiltrate, discredit, and disrupt lawful domestic political groups. Its own directive language, quoted in the exposed files and the congressional record, was to "**expose, disrupt, misdirect, discredit, or otherwise neutralize**" its targets — among them the civil-rights movement and **Martin Luther King Jr.**, the Black Panther Party, the Socialist Workers Party, and the anti-war movement. It stayed hidden until **8 March 1971**, when the Citizens' Commission to Investigate the FBI **burglarized the Bureau's Media, Pennsylvania office** and mailed the files to the press — the exposure that helped precipitate the Church Committee, whose **Book III** condemned it as "a sophisticated vigilante operation aimed squarely at preventing the exercise of First Amendment rights."

## Asserted intent — firewalled
The documented program is the disruption of lawful political activity. Specific downstream intent-claims — that COINTELPRO orchestrated particular deaths — are separate, attributed, and mostly weak or contested; "the program existed and targeted King" is kept apart from any specific assassination-direction claim, which would need its own primary and its own named claimant.

## Held-open
Sources note COINTELPRO was roughly "0.2% of the FBI's overall workload" — a real datum that cuts against a "the whole Bureau was this" reading. Recorded; it neither excuses nor inflates. The documented fact is the program, its directive, and its exposure.

## Role in the thesis
The domestic sibling of the rail's over-tidyings: a real covert program, concealed for fifteen years and surfaced only by a burglary — the kind of documented secrecy that, once revealed, makes every later suspicion feel already confirmed.
