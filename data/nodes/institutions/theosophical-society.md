# Theosophical Society

> **WELD** · map node 1.5 · 1875-

The institution the esoteric idiom runs through — the body that the four-idiom nodes (Besant, Bailey, the world-teacher) all descend into or break away from.

## Documented connection
The Theosophical Society was founded in **New York City on 17 November 1875** by **Helena Blavatsky**, **Colonel Henry Steel Olcott** (its first President), and **William Quan Judge**, with sixteen others. It declared **three Objects** — universal brotherhood "without distinction of race, creed, sex, caste or color"; the comparative study of religion, philosophy, and science; and the investigation of "unexplained laws of nature and the powers latent in humanity." The founders moved the work to India, taking up residence at **Adyar, Madras, on 19 December 1882**; the **Adyar Library** followed in 1886. From Adyar the Society became the institutional stage for the events the articulation rail already documents: Leadbeater's 1909 identification of Krishnamurti, Besant's Order of the Star, and the three schisms that carried theosophy outward — **Judge** (the 1895 American split), **Steiner** (out to found Anthroposophy, 1913), and **Bailey** (out to the Lucis Trust, 1920).

## Asserted intent — firewalled
That the Masters/Mahatmas Blavatsky named were **real adepts steering human evolution** is her own attributed doctrine, not a documented fact — the theosophical form of the elect-administering claim, quarantined exactly as the Crowley rail quarantines the "Secret Chiefs." The Society is a documented institution; the cosmic hierarchy it taught is a belief it held, held here as such.

## Held-open
- The founding is dated to a **September 1875** first organizing meeting and the **17 November 1875** formal inauguration; both recorded, neither collapsed.
- The Adyar dates are two events, not one: **residence 19 Dec 1882**, **Library 1886**.

## Role in the thesis
The hub of the esoteric idiom: not a lineage that transmits a program down a chain, but an institution people **joined and then left** — a nucleus that kept fissioning. The recurrence of the managed-child claim across Besant, Steiner, and Bailey is documented; that it was directed from one center is exactly what the schisms disprove.
