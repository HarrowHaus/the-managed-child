# Society for Psychical Research

> **WELD** · map node 2.1 · 1882-

The founding body of organized psychical research — and, in this study, the documented investigator that examined the esoteric idiom and could not settle it either.

## Documented connection
The Society for Psychical Research was founded in London in **1882** by Cambridge figures including **Henry Sidgwick**, **Frederic W. H. Myers**, and **Edmund Gurney**, to test claims of the paranormal by the standards of evidence. Its most consequential intersection with this study is the **1885 Hodgson report**: the SPR sent **Richard Hodgson** to Adyar, and its *Proceedings* published his conclusion that **Helena Blavatsky** had written the "Mahatma Letters" herself — the committee's verdict, that she was "one of the most accomplished, ingenious, and interesting impostors in history."

## Held-open
The SPR's own verdict did not hold: in **1986** its *Journal* published **Vernon Harrison**'s finding that the Hodgson report was unsound, and the Society stated that Blavatsky "was unjustly condemned." The body that condemned her later un-condemned her; both findings are on its own record, and this node keeps both.

## Role in the thesis
The evidence-standard applied to the esoteric idiom from outside — and a caution the study takes to heart. The SPR is where a documented institution tried to adjudicate a belief-claim and produced first a fraud finding, then its retraction: proof that even the careful investigation of these matters resolves, here, into a seam kept open rather than a verdict picked.
