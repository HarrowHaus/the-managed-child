# Society for Psychical Research

> **WELD** · map node 2.1 · 1882-

The founding body of organized psychical research — and, in this study, the documented investigator that examined the esoteric idiom and could not settle it either.

## Documented connection
The Society for Psychical Research was founded in London in **1882** by Cambridge figures including **Henry Sidgwick**, **Frederic W. H. Myers**, and **Edmund Gurney**, to test claims of the paranormal by the standards of evidence. Its most consequential intersection with this study is the **1885 Hodgson report**: the SPR sent **Richard Hodgson** to Adyar, and its *Proceedings* published his conclusion that **Helena Blavatsky** had written the "Mahatma Letters" herself — the committee's verdict, that she was "one of the most accomplished, ingenious, and interesting impostors in history."

## Held-open
A century later the Hodgson report was sharply criticized — but it is important to state what did **not** happen. In April **1986** the SPR's *Journal* published **Vernon Harrison**'s "J'Accuse," finding the 1885 report "riddled with slanted statements … and downright falsity"; Harrison's own verdict, though, was the Scots **"not proven,"** not a declaration of innocence, and he expanded the critique into a 1997 monograph. The **SPR holds no corporate opinions** and issued no institutional reversal: the widely-quoted line that Blavatsky "was unjustly condemned" was the headline of a 1986 SPR **press release** announcing Harrison's study, not a verdict of the Society. Both the 1885 report and the 1986 critique are the work of **individual investigators published under SPR auspices** — the node keeps the tension without inventing a corporate flip.

## Role in the thesis
The evidence-standard applied to the esoteric idiom from outside — and a caution the study takes to heart. The SPR is where a documented institution's members tried to adjudicate a belief-claim and produced first a fraud finding, then, a century later, a fellow member's rebuttal of it — never a corporate verdict either way: proof that even the careful investigation of these matters resolves, here, into a seam kept open rather than a verdict picked.
