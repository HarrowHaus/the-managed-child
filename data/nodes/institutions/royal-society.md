# The Royal Society

> **WELD** · map node Cap.1e · founded 1660

Bacon's fable made an institution.

## Documented core [WELD]
Founded in **1660** and chartered by the Crown in **1662**, the Royal Society took Bacon's *New Atlantis* as a working ideal; its early apologist **Thomas Sprat** (*History of the Royal-Society*, 1667) framed the Society as the realisation of Salomon's House. The elect of philosopher-scientists that Bacon imagined became a standing body — the robes exchanged for instruments, the structure held.

## Role in the thesis
The documented step at which the trunk's scientific idiom stopped being a fable and became an institution. Cited as the ordinary reading-and-adoption of a published ideal, not as a secret order.
