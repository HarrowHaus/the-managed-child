## TL;DR
- The documented spine holds at full strength and is almost entirely primary-at-source (participant testimony, band history, obituary, court-adjacent record): **Ken Kesey and Robert Hunter took their first psychedelics as PAID SUBJECTS in government-connected drug studies at the Menlo Park VA Hospital / Stanford (c. 1959–62)** → Kesey dispersed it through **the Merry Pranksters' Acid Tests (1965–66)**, mass public LSD parties → **the Warlocks became the Grateful Dead and were the Acid Tests' paid house band ($100/week)** → **Owsley "Bear" Stanley** supplied the acid AND financed/engineered the early Dead. - **The load-bearing contested hop is the very first one.** That Kesey's specific Menlo Park VA study was formally a **numbered MKUltra subproject** is NOT firmly primary-documented — it is a widely-repeated attribution that resolves DOWN under scrutiny. Kesey's own account is the primary that he was a paid subject who first took LSD there; the CIA/MKUltra *funding label* on that particular Menlo Park study is asserted by popular and semi-scholarly sources (via Gottlieb's documented bogus-foundation conduits) but contradicted by at least one institutional source (Stanford's own alumni magazine calls it "Army-sponsored," 1961) and is not tied to a located subproject number. Grade the connection `documented`; grade the MKUltra-subproject attribution `HYPOTHESIS`/contested.
- **Every intent/causation edge ("the CIA engineered the counterculture") is separate, weaker, and firewalled behind its named claimant.** The strongest responsible version is scholarly and deliberately weak: Martin A. Hank Harrison's early-Dead role is self-described and disputed (grade the *degree*); the Courtney Love tie is documented familial only.
- Expect a high exception count: the Menlo funding label, the 1959-vs-1960-vs-1961 date, the "unwitting midwife" vs "engineered it" seam, and Harrison's self-inflated role are all live seams. That is the rail working correctly (R-7). **This rail is the project's model case of documented reverberation with the intent kept firewalled.**

## Key Findings

**The connection-edges are strong and largely participant-primary.** Kesey really was a paid drug-study subject at the Menlo Park VA who took LSD there and then dispersed it; the Acid Tests really were mass public LSD events (the Trips Festival drew ~10,000 in January 1966); the Warlocks really became the Grateful Dead and were paid to be the Tests' house band; Owsley really produced millions of doses of the purest underground LSD, supplied the Tests, financed the Dead, and built their sound system. All of that can be stated flat.

**The intent-edges are weaker, scaled, and always belong to a named claimant.** No documented dosing licenses "therefore the state seeded the counterculture on purpose." The chain from "MKUltra's LSD physically reached Kesey" (near-documented) to "the CIA deliberately engineered the counterculture" (apocryphal) passes through exactly the firewall this rail exists to hold: Lee/Shlain's "unwitting midwife" (careful, scholarly) is one thing; McGowan's coordinated-psyop thesis (synthesizer, savaged) is another; they must not inherit each other's standing.

### Anchor 1 — Ken Kesey: the paid subject who dispersed it (THE LOAD-BEARING CONTESTED HOP)
**Connection [primary — participant account]:** Ken Kesey, then a Stanford creative-writing graduate student living on Perry Lane, volunteered as a **paid subject** in a psychoactive-drug study at the **Menlo Park Veterans' Administration Hospital**, where he also worked as a night aide. He was invited by his Perry Lane neighbor **Vic Lovell** (a Stanford psychology graduate student). He was paid **$75 per session**; the study administered LSD, psilocybin, mescaline, and other hallucinogens. His experience as a subject and as a ward aide directly inspired *One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest*. This — that Kesey first took LSD as a paid government-study subject and then became its most effective mass evangelist — is the documented core, and Kesey's own account (relayed in Tom Wolfe's *The Electric Kool-Aid Acid Test* and Kesey's many interviews) is the primary. [SECONDARY, orientation] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ken_Kesey ; [SECONDARY] https://stanfordmag.org/contents/what-a-trip
**Connection [primary — participant account, corroborating]:** Future Grateful Dead lyricist **Robert Hunter** was a subject in the same milieu — paid to take LSD, psilocybin, and mescaline in a Stanford-connected study (c. 1962), paid ~**$140**. Hunter's verbatim account: "At first, they gave me LSD, then the next week, I think it was mescaline, the next week it was psilocybin, and the fourth week it was all three at once." [SECONDARY] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Hunter_(lyricist) ; https://faroutmagazine.co.uk/robert-hunter-and-the-cia-how-mk-ultra-gave-the-grateful-dead-their-voice/
**Intent/causation edge — the MKUltra-subproject attribution [CONTESTED / HYPOTHESIS — the pin the operator asked for]:** The claim that Kesey's *particular* Menlo Park VA study was formally an **MKUltra subproject** is repeated nearly everywhere (Wikipedia: "turned out to be a CIA-financed study under the aegis of Project MKULTRA"; NPR/Kinzer; Collectors Weekly: "the CIA's notorious MKUltra program") and rests on a real mechanism — Gottlieb documentedly routed MKUltra money to "colleges, universities, research foundations, hospitals, clinics, and penal institutions" through **bogus philanthropic foundations**, so that the on-site researchers "had no idea that their research was actually being funded by the CIA." **But the direct documentary tie of the Menlo Park VA study to a located, numbered subproject is not established, and the label is not unanimous:** Stanford's own alumni magazine calls it "an **Army-sponsored** hallucinogenic-drug experiment" and dates Kesey's enrollment to **1961**, with no mention of the CIA or MKUltra at all. Wikipedia itself hedges ("supposedly without his knowledge"). The study's on-site lead is generally named as **Dr. Leo Hollister** (a real VA psychopharmacologist whose Menlo Park LSD/psychotomimetic work is independently documented); whether Hollister's funding traced to MKUltra, to the Army, or to the VA/NIMH is exactly what the primary record does not cleanly settle. **Grade the connection (Kesey was a paid subject who first took LSD at Menlo Park and dispersed it) `documented`/WELD on his own account; grade the "it was an MKUltra subproject" attribution `HYPOTHESIS`/contested — R-2 resolves it down.**
**Grade-exception (date + funding-label, kept open R-7):** the enrollment year is given as **1959** (most popular sources), **1960** (some), and **1961** (Stanford Magazine); the funder as **CIA/MKUltra** (popular/semi-scholarly) vs **Army** (Stanford Magazine) vs unspecified **VA/NIMH**. Record all; pick none. The load-bearing honest sentence: *MKUltra's LSD-dispersal apparatus is documented; that this specific study was one of its subprojects is asserted, plausible via the foundation conduit, and not primary-nailed.*

### Anchor 2 — The Merry Pranksters / the Acid Tests (1965–66): mass public dispersal
**Connection [primary — event record + participant accounts]:** Kesey and his **Merry Pranksters** staged the **Acid Tests**, a series of freeform multimedia LSD parties in the San Francisco Bay Area, **late 1965 into 1966**. The name was Kesey's; posters read **"Can you pass the acid test?"** The first is generally dated to a Prankster gathering near Santa Cruz on **27 November 1965**; the Tests then went public and expanded onto college campuses, making LSD (still legal until October 1966) broadly accessible. The **Trips Festival** (Longshoreman's Hall, San Francisco, **January 1966**), co-organized by Kesey, Stewart Brand, Ramon Sender, and Owsley Stanley, drew a sold-out **~10,000 people** across three nights. The sequence closed with the **Acid Test Graduation** (a SoMa warehouse, **31 October 1966**). [SECONDARY, orientation] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acid_Tests ; https://origins.osu.edu/milestones/december-2015-acid-tests
**Intent/causation edge:** That the Acid Tests were a witting continuation of a state program is the unlicensed leap; the documented fact is a private mass-dispersal movement whose *seed* (Kesey's first dose) came from a government-connected study. The reverberation is the finding; the direction (top-down design) is not.
**Grade-exception (first-Test date/venue):** sources split on the very first Test's location (Ken Babbs' house / a home near Santa Cruz / Soquel) and whether the Warlocks actually played it (several witnesses say they did not play a set at the first gathering). Record; do not resolve.

### Anchor 3 — The Warlocks → the Grateful Dead: the paid house band
**Connection [primary — band history]:** **The Warlocks** debuted publicly **5 May 1965** at Magoo's Pizza Parlor in Menlo Park; by early 1966 they had renamed themselves **the Grateful Dead** and were the **Acid Tests' house band**, the only proper band to play the Tests regularly, **paid $100 a week by the Pranksters** to do so. The Tests were their formative training ground for improvisational, long-form performance. [SECONDARY, orientation] https://relix.com/articles/detail/55-years-ago-today-the-grateful-dead-debuted-how-the-warlocks-passed-the-acid-tests/ ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acid_Tests
**Connection [documented proximity — closes the loop to Anchor 1]:** the Dead's own lyricist (Hunter) and their patron-adjacent circle came directly out of the same Menlo Park / Stanford drug-study milieu — the band that scored the mass dispersal was, at the roots, downstream of the paid-subject studies. State-lab → paid subjects → the house band of the movement: the whole rail in one hop.
**Intent/causation edge:** none strong here; the tie is straightforwardly documented. Keep it flat.

### Anchor 4 — Owsley "Bear" Stanley: supplied the acid, financed the band
**Connection [primary — biography + band history]:** **Augustus Owsley Stanley III** (1935–2011), clandestine chemist, produced an estimated **~500 grams / ~5 million doses** of the purest underground LSD **1965–67** (first lysergic-acid shipment 30 March 1965; the "White Lightning" and later batches). He became the **primary LSD supplier to Kesey and the Pranksters from ~September 1965** and supplied the Acid Tests (he provided the acid at the Watts Acid Test, 12 February 1966). He then **both financed the Grateful Dead and served as their first sound engineer**, later lead-designing the **Wall of Sound** (1973) and recording the band's "sonic journals." He co-designed the skull-and-lightning logo. [SECONDARY, orientation] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Owsley_Stanley ; https://www.rollingstone.com/feature/owsley-stanley-the-king-of-lsd-82181/
**Intent/causation edge:** A recurring internet claim that Owsley's precursor or product was itself CIA-routed is NOT documented — his lab and chemistry are independently sourced as private/clandestine. Do not let "MKUltra bought the early LSD supply" (true of Gottlieb's 1950s Sandoz purchase) leak into "Owsley's 1965 acid was CIA acid" (unsupported). Two different supplies, a decade apart.
**Grade-exception (dose/quantity figures):** production estimates vary (1.25 million doses per some sources; ~5 million per Wikipedia's gram-count). Record the range; do not pick.

### Anchor 5 — Hank Harrison → Courtney Love: the disputed-degree tie

He **described himself** as one of the band's first personal managers / roadies. **The degree is contested:** official band histories attribute early management to **Rock Scully**, and Scully characterized Harrison as **a liar** who recounted events he did not attend; his role is generally portrayed as peripheral/informal. **Connection [documented familial]:** Harrison is the **biological father of musician Courtney Love**, born during his marriage to **Linda Carroll** (née Risi), which ended in divorce c. 1966. This is universally recognized and documented. Love has alleged Harrison **gave her LSD as a young child**; Harrison has **consistently denied** it and at one point threatened litigation over the LSD allegations. [SECONDARY] https://blabbermouth.net/news/courtney-love-s-father-threatens-to-sue-over-lsd-allegations
**Intent/causation edge [firewalled]:** any reading that the Harrison→Love familial line is evidence of a designed multigenerational program is unsupported synthesis; the documented content is (a) Harrison's real-but-disputed proximity to the early Dead and (b) the plain familial fact. **Grade-exception:** Harrison is himself a minor synthesizer (he later authored conspiracy-tinged books about Kurt Cobain's death). Keep his Dead-orbit *fact* apart from his *self-narration* and apart from his later claim-making.

## Caveats
- **The load-bearing contested hop, stated plainly (the operator's asked-for pin):** *How firmly does the Menlo Park study tie to MKUltra?* — **Not firmly.** The DOCUMENTED facts are: Kesey (and Hunter) were paid subjects in psychedelic drug studies at the Menlo Park VA / Stanford c. 1959–62 (participant primary); MKUltra documentedly funneled money to hospitals and clinics through bogus foundations so on-site researchers didn't know the source (Gottlieb-mechanism, documented). - **Contradictions kept open, not resolved:** Menlo funder (CIA/MKUltra vs Army vs VA/NIMH); Kesey enrollment year (1959/1960/1961); first-Acid-Test venue/date and whether the Warlocks played it; Owsley's dose count (~1.25M vs ~5M); Harrison's early-Dead role (self-described "first manager" vs Rock Scully's "liar"); the Love/Harrison LSD-to-child accusation (asserted vs denied).
