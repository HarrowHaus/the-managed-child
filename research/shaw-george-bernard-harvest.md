## TL;DR
- **Shaw is a genuine founding-generation Fabian, documented.** He attended the Fabian Society's meeting of **16 May 1884**, joined **September 1884** (sources vary; executive committee from **1885**, serving to **1911**), and became its foremost pamphleteer and public voice. He **edited *Fabian Essays in Socialism* (Fabian Society, December 1889)**, wrote its **Preface** and **two of the eight essays** ("Economic"; "Transition"). This is daylight, not conspiracy-sourced. **Grade the node WELD.**
- **The named "permeation" boast is Shaw's, and it is 1892 — NOT the 1889 Essays (L2 trap).** In **Fabian Tract No. 41, *The Fabian Society: What It Has Done and How It Has Done It*** — a paper read at Essex Hall, **6 February 1892** — Shaw wrote: **"We permeated the party organizations and pulled all the wires we could lay our hands on with our utmost adroitness and energy."** Date it to **1892**. The 1889 Essays state gradualism-as-doctrine; the *word* "permeation" and the wire-pulling boast are three years later.
- **Shaw's eugenics is REAL, in his OWN printed words, and it belongs to 1903+ — NOT the 1889 Essays (R-3, L2).** The strongest primary is his own text: **"The only fundamental and possible Socialism is the socialization of the selective breeding of Man: in other terms, of human evolution"** — *The Revolutionist's Handbook and Pocket Companion*, the appendix to **Man and Superman (1903)**, printed under the fictional authorship of "John Tanner." This is a genuine Shaw line and it is quotable at WELD. Do **not** attach it to `fabian-essays`; attach it to Shaw the person and to the eugenics cluster.
- **The "lethal chamber" material is real but must be handled precisely — it is a PRESS REPORT of a spoken lecture, and its register is provocation (R-2, R-7).** Shaw's much-quoted **"A part of eugenic politics would finally land us in an extensive use of the lethal chamber. A great many people would have to be put out of existence simply because it wastes other people's time to look after them"** comes from his **1910 lecture to the Eugenics Education Society, as reported in *The Daily Express*, 4 March 1910** — a newspaper account of spoken remarks, not a text Shaw wrote and signed. The **1934** "humane gas" line — advocate discovering **"a humane gas that will kill instantly and painlessly … deadly by all means, but humane"** — is on record in **The Listener** (BBC), Feb 1934, and in his filmed remarks. Report these attributed to their date and medium; flag that Shaw's register was deliberately shocking-satirical Shavian provocation, and that the press-report standing is weaker than his own printed *Man and Superman* line. The moat wants the printed line as the load-bearing eugenics primary.
- **The superman / Life-Force theme is documented across *Man and Superman* (1903) and *Back to Methuselah* (1921).** Creative Evolution guided toward the Superman — "We must replace the man by the superman" (*Revolutionist's Handbook*) — is the managed-improvement idiom in Shaw's own hand. ## The PRIMARIES

### 1. Shaw as Fabian founder and editor [PRIMARY / near-primary — biographical, cross-checked]

- **Fabian Society membership.** Shaw attended a Fabian Society meeting on **16 May 1884**, and joined the Society in **1884** (September; sources also give the first-attendance date). He sat on its **Executive Committee from 1885 to 1911** and became its leading pamphleteer. [documented — Spartacus Educational; Online Library of Liberty ("Shaw and the Fabian Society"); standard biography]
- **Editor of *Fabian Essays in Socialism*.** Shaw **edited** the volume (Fabian Society, London, **December 1889**), wrote its **Preface** (English ed.), and authored **two of the eight essays** — "Economic" (in *The Basis of Socialism*) and "Transition" (in *The Transition to Social Democracy*). Public-domain full text: Internet Archive (`fabianessaysinso00fabirich`), Project Gutenberg #69088, Econlib. [documented — see the built node `fabian-essays`; cross-check that harvest]
- **Fabian Manifesto (Tract No. 2, 1884).** Shaw drafted the Society's early *Manifesto* (Fabian Tract No. 2, 1884). [documented — Online Library of Liberty]

### 2. The permeation boast — Fabian Tract No. 41 (1892) [PRIMARY — verbatim, dated] — the L2 dating spine

**Full title:** *The Fabian Society: What It Has Done and How It Has Done It.*
**Author:** **G. Bernard Shaw.**
**Occasion/date:** a paper **read at a Conference of the London and Provincial Fabian Societies at Essex Hall, 6 February 1892**, ordered printed for members as **Fabian Tract No. 41**. Full text: Internet Archive (`TheFabianSocietySocialismAndAgriculture` / `TheFabianSocietyItsEarlyHistory`).

- The named-permeation boast, verbatim: **"We permeated the party organizations and pulled all the wires we could lay our hands on with our utmost adroitness and energy."** (Shaw goes on to credit the Fabians with putting ideas into the heads of the 1888 Progressive majority on the first London County Council.)

**L2 discipline:** this is **1892**, three years after the *Essays*. The *word* "permeation" and the wire-pulling boast are here, not in the 1889 volume. Both the built `fabian-essays` node and `permeation.md` already keep this date clean; Shaw's node must too. (Sidney Webb's motto **"the inevitability of gradualness"** is a third, still-later date — his **26 June 1923** Labour Party address, Fabian Tract 207 — and is Webb's, not Shaw's; keep it off Shaw entirely.)

### 3. Eugenics in Shaw's own words — *Man and Superman* / *The Revolutionist's Handbook* (1903) [PRIMARY — verbatim, printed text]

**Man and Superman: A Comedy and a Philosophy** — written 1901–1903, **published 1903** (Constable). Its appendix, **"The Revolutionist's Handbook and Pocket Companion,"** is printed under the fictional authorship of the play's character **John Tanner** — a Shavian device, but the doctrine is Shaw's and he claimed it. Full text: Project Gutenberg; Wikisource (`Man_and_Superman/The_Revolutionist's_Handbook`).

- **The load-bearing eugenics line, verbatim:** **"The only fundamental and possible Socialism is the socialization of the selective breeding of Man: in other terms, of human evolution."** (*The Revolutionist's Handbook*.)
- The political-superman frame: **"we shall certainly come again [to catastrophe] unless we can have a Democracy of Supermen"**; and **"Our only hope, then, is in evolution. We must replace the man by the superman."**

This is the **cleanest eugenics primary** for the node — Shaw's own printed words, public-domain, quotable at WELD. It fuses the socialist-collectivist register with the breeding-the-stock register in one sentence. **This is 1903, not 1889** — it is NOT in *Fabian Essays* and must never be attached to that work (R-3).

### 4. The "lethal chamber" / eugenic-killing rhetoric — spoken/reported, 1910 and 1934 [near-primary — PRESS/BROADCAST, handle precisely]

Much-quoted, often distorted. What Shaw ACTUALLY said, with its real medium and date:

- **1910 — Eugenics Education Society lecture (press report):** **"A part of eugenic politics would finally land us in an extensive use of the lethal chamber. A great many people would have to be put out of existence simply because it wastes other people's time to look after them."** — reported in **The Daily Express, 4 March 1910** (his spoken lecture to London's Eugenics Education Society), reproduced in Evelyn Cobley, *Modernism and the Culture of Efficiency* (Univ. of Toronto Press, 2009), p. 159. **This is a newspaper report of spoken remarks, not a Shaw-authored, Shaw-signed text** — its standing is weaker than the printed *Man and Superman* line (R-2). Attach as attributed press report, dated.
- **1931 — filmed interview (Paramount British Pictures, 5 March 1931):** **"I don't want to punish anybody, but there are an extraordinary number of people who I want to kill."** — on-the-record filmed remark, deliberately provocative.
- **1934 — broadcast, printed in *The Listener* (BBC), Feb 1934:** an appeal for chemists to discover **"a humane gas that will kill instantly and painlessly … deadly by all means, but humane, not cruel."** — on-record, dated.

**R-7 / register caution (do NOT drop it, do NOT let it swallow the node):** Shaw's mode here is Shavian shock-provocation — the professional contrarian's pose of saying the unsayable to scandalize the respectable. That register is part of the honest account; it neither excuses nor erases the content. Report the words at strength (R-5), attributed and dated, and note the register — never launder it into "he didn't mean it," never inflate a satirical press line into a settled policy program. The printed 1903 line is the load-bearing eugenics primary; the lethal-chamber material is corroborating, at press/broadcast standing.

### 5. The superman / Life-Force theme — *Back to Methuselah* (1921) [PRIMARY — text]

**Back to Methuselah: A Metabiological Pentateuch** — five plays + the preface **"An Infidel Half-Century,"** written 1918–20, **published 1921** (Constable, London; Brentano's, NY). Shaw's systematic statement of **Creative Evolution** (the Lamarckian/Bergsonian *élan vital*, not Darwinian natural selection) driving humanity toward longer life and the Superman — the managed-improvement idiom given "systematic philosophic form" (his own framing) as the sequel to *Man and Superman*. Real, quotable, WELD for the superman/creative-evolution theme. Full text: Standard Ebooks; Project Gutenberg.

### 6. The 1930s dictatorship praise [documented — attributed, dated; FIREWALL, kept precise]

- **1931 USSR visit:** Shaw made a **nine-day** trip, met **Stalin** for about three hours (with Lord and Lady Astor). Afterward he called Stalin a **"giant"** while Western statesmen were **"pigmies"**; at a Moscow dinner he said **"I have seen the 'terrors' and I was terribly pleased by them"**; leaving, **"Tomorrow I leave this land of hope and return to our Western countries — the countries of despair."** [documented — Russia Beyond; Cambridge, *George Bernard Shaw in Context*, ch. "Totalitarianism"]
- **Broader 1930s:** Shaw defended dictatorship and at various points expressed admiration for **Mussolini, Stalin, and even Hitler** as strong men who got things done — a well-attested and much-discussed feature of late Shaw among Shavians. [documented — Cambridge, *Shaw in Context*; secondary literature]

**R-3 / R-7 discipline:** this is a genuine, documented firewall item and belongs in the node's "held-open" register, dated and attributed — the same trained-elite disposition curdled into strong-man worship in old age. It is a late symptom, not the node's center; do not let it retroactively recolor the 1884–1889 Fabian articulation (which is the node's map-0.2 job), and do not soften it either.
