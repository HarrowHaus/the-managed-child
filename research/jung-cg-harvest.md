## The PRIMARIES — Jung's substrate corpus (thesis-relevant slice only)

*This is the THESIS slice — the universal-inherited-archetype claim and the divine-child archetype — NOT the whole of Jung's psychology (typology, individuation, synchronicity, alchemy per se are out of scope except as noted).*

**1. *Psychological Types* (1921).** German *Psychologische Typen* (Rascher Verlag, Zurich, 1921); CW vol. 6. Best known for introversion/extraversion, but thesis-relevant for its statement of the **primordial image**:
- *"The great problems of life … are always related to the primordial images of the collective unconscious. These images … are deposits representing the accumulated experience of thousands of years."* (Ch. V.) — the archetype as **inherited deposit of collective experience**.

**2. *Modern Man in Search of a Soul* (1933).** English collection, Kegan Paul / Harcourt Brace, **1933** (trans. Dell & Baynes) — Jung's most-read popular book, the vector by which the archetype idiom reached the general Anglophone reader:
- *"Every civilized human being, whatever his conscious development, is still an archaic man at the deeper levels of his psyche."* — the **"archaic remnants"** thesis in plain words.
- *"The dream is the small hidden door in the deepest and most intimate sanctum of the soul, which opens to that primeval cosmic night that was soul long before there was conscious ego …"*

**3. *The Psychology of the Child Archetype* (with Karl Kerényi, 1940).** Jung's essay *Zur Psychologie des Kindarchetypus*, paired with Kerényi's *Das göttliche Kind* ("The Divine Child") in *Essays on a Science of Mythology* / *Das göttliche Kind* (1940; Eng. *Essays on a Science of Mythology* / *The Science of Mythology*, Bollingen); later collected in **CW 9i**. This is the **documented primary that ties Jung directly to the DIVINE-CHILD / child archetype** — the load-bearing text for the `aeon-of-the-child` analogy and for the node's child register. (Kerényi is himself an Eranos figure — the collaboration is an Eranos-circle product.)

**4. *The Archetypes and the Collective Unconscious* (CW 9, Part 1).** Princeton/Bollingen, trans. R. F. C. Hull; the **volume** appeared **1959**, gathering essays written across the 1930s–50s. The canonical statements of the thesis (verbatim, quoted short, fair-use):
- *"But this personal layer rests upon a deeper layer, which does not derive from personal experience and is not a personal acquisition but is inborn. This deeper layer I call the 'collective unconscious' … this part of the unconscious is not individual but universal … it has contents and modes of behaviour that are more or less the same everywhere and in all individuals."* (¶3–4.)
- *"the content of the collective unconscious is made up essentially of 'archetypes' … there exists a second psychic system of a collective, universal, and impersonal nature which is identical in all individuals."* (¶42–43.)
- **The FORM-not-content guard (L2 — cite this to keep the node honest):** *"The representations themselves are not inherited, only the forms, and in that respect they correspond in every way to the instincts, which are also determined in form only."* (¶155.) Jung does **not** claim inherited images; he claims inherited *structuring forms*. A node that flattens this to "inherited memories" over-claims the primary.

## Eranos — the network node (documented)

- **Founded** in **Ascona (Ticino), Switzerland**; the annual lecture program began **August 1933**, at the estate ("Casa Gabriella") of **Olga Fröbe-Kapteyn (1881–1962)**, a Dutch-English theosophically-formed patron. She had built a lecture hall c.1928; **Jung** had suggested she make it a "meeting place between East and West." The name **Eranos** (Greek: a shared meal to which each contributes) was proposed by the religious scholar **Rudolf Otto**.
- **Jung was the gravitational center** — a near-annual lecturer through the 1930s–40s. The recurring circle (the "dancers" who returned yearly) included **Karl Kerényi** (classical philology / myth), **Gershom Scholem** (Jewish mysticism / Kabbalah), **Henry Corbin** (Islamic / Iranian esotericism), **Mircea Eliade** (history of religions; first Eranos lecture 1950 — see `eliade-mircea`, a stub), **Heinrich Zimmer** (Indology), **D. T. Suzuki** (Zen), and later **Joseph Campbell**.
- **Thesis role:** Eranos is the documented milieu where Jungian depth-psychology, comparative religion, and myth-scholarship fused into the mid-century "sacred/archetypal" idiom. It is a `network-exposure` / `institutional-relay` node, NOT a cabal (min-proof met: documented co-presence + the ideas documented as circulating; does-not-permit: reading co-attendance as agreement or coordination). **No `eranos` or `froebe-kapteyn` node exists** — carry in prose; flag for possible future minting.

## Bollingen — the institutional relay to American elite culture (documented)

- The **Bollingen Series** was **inaugurated in 1943**, as a program of the **Old Dominion Foundation** (which **Paul Mellon** founded in 1941). The **Bollingen Foundation** proper was established **1945**. It was **named after "Bollingen Tower,"** the castle-like retreat Jung built at Bollingen on Lake Zurich.
- It was **conceived by Paul and his wife Mary Conover Mellon expressly to publish Jung's *Collected Works* in English** (Mary Conover Mellon's particular interest). The **Collected Works of C. G. Jung** = **Bollingen Series 20**, **21 volumes over ~26 years** (trans. R. F. C. Hull).
- **The same series carried the corpus's other spine-nodes:** **Campbell's *The Hero with a Thousand Faces* = Bollingen Series XVII (1949)** (already WELD in the corpus, `works/hero-thousand-faces.md`), and the **Eranos yearbook papers = Bollingen Series XXX** (6 vols., *Spirit and Nature* 1954 … *The Mystic Vision* 1969), **selected/edited by Joseph Campbell** from Fröbe-Kapteyn's *Eranos-Jahrbücher*.
- The Foundation became inactive in **1968**; its list passed to **Princeton University Press**.
- **Thesis role:** Bollingen is the documented `institutional-relay` — Jungian myth reaching mid-century American publishing under elite philanthropic patronage (Mellon), the same channel that carried Campbell and Eranos. Min-proof met (both parties documented in the institution AND the institution documented carrying the content); does-not-permit: reading Mellon patronage as a plot. **No `bollingen` node exists** — carry in prose; flag.

## The charged material, handled honestly (R-3 / R-8; peripheral, dated, OFF-CENTER)

### (a) The Nazi-era presidency + *Zentralblatt* (antisemitism charge) — attributed, contested, dated
- **The facts:** In **June 1933**, after the Nazis' rise, Jung — then honorary vice-president — **accepted the presidency of the (reorganized) General Medical Society for Psychotherapy**, and was **editor of its journal, the *Zentralblatt für Psychotherapie***. He served until **1939** (resigning the presidency; the *Zentralblatt* editorship lapsing c.1940). Jung's defenders note he **restructured the Society as the *International* General Medical Society for Psychotherapy so that German Jewish practitioners, barred from the national section, could join as individual members** — a documented structural fact.
- **The charge:** a **December 1933 *Zentralblatt*** editorial distinguished "Aryan" from "Jewish" psychology (and, in the surrounding numbers, praise of Nazi ideology appeared under **Matthias Göring**'s co-editorship — Göring a cousin of Hermann Göring). Jung's 1934 essay *"The State of Psychotherapy Today"* contains the contested lines contrasting the "Aryan" and "Jewish" unconscious. Jung **claimed** the Nazi-praising material ran without his authorization; critics note he **never published a repudiating editorial**.
- **Standing (R-3 split):** the *facts* (presidency 1933–39, editorship, the 1933 editorial, the 1934 essay) are **documented and dated**; the *character claim* ("Jung was an antisemite / Nazi collaborator") is **attributed and CONTESTED** — argued by critics, rebutted by defenders (e.g., Andrew Samuels' scholarship parses it; the reorganization-to-protect-Jewish-members point cuts the other way). Per R-8, keep it **behind its claimants and their basis**; per R-3, split *the acts happened* from *the intent/character*. **Do NOT center it; do NOT launder it.** It does not touch the WELD on the archetype corpus.

### (b) The occult / "Jung-as-magus" reading — attributed, precise, off-center
- Rests on the ***Red Book / Liber Novus*** (composed **1914–1930** via "active imagination," published only in **2009**), the inner guide-figure **Philemon** (read by esotericists as a "Holy Guardian Angel"), Jung's associations with Swiss occult circles and the **Monte Verità** milieu near Ascona, and his lifelong study of **alchemy** (*Psychology and Alchemy*, CW 12). Occult readers treat *Liber Novus* as "a primary occult text for the modern era."
- **Standing:** a **real esoteric strand of Jung's biography** (documented: the Red Book exists, Philemon, the alchemy studies) — but the reading of Jung as a *magus / conscious occult operator* is **interpretive/attributed**, `analogy` at most, and **off-center** for this node. Keep precise; do not let it become the node's spine, and do not import conspiracy framing (R-1).
