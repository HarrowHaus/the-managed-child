## TL;DR
- The documented spine holds at full strength and is almost entirely institutional/congressional record, declassified primary, court filing, and FOIA-recovered paper. **The government really did do the things**: the **Church Committee** (US Senate Select Committee to Study Governmental Operations with Respect to Intelligence Activities, 1975–76) documented CIA assassination plots, FBI COINTELPRO, NSA SHAMROCK/MINARET, and MKUltra across a ~700-page final report in six books; **Operation Northwoods** (Joint Chiefs memo, 13 March 1962) really proposed manufacturing pretexts — staged attacks, faked airliner shootdowns, sinking Cuban-refugee boats — to justify invading Cuba; **COINTELPRO** (FBI, 1956–71) really ran a covert program to "expose, disrupt, misdirect, discredit, or otherwise neutralize" lawful domestic groups; and the **NSA's own 2005-declassified historical study** (Robert Hanyok) found the 4 August 1964 **Gulf of Tonkin** "second attack" did not happen and that SIGINT contradicting it was omitted.
- **The point is the over-tidying, not any single conspiracy.** Northwoods was a *rejected* proposal (Kennedy killed it); Tonkin was, on Hanyok's own reading, a SIGINT-handling failure "skewed" toward a predetermined claim, **not proven to be a deliberate top-level lie**; the Church Committee is itself the documented *correction*, not a cover-up. What each supplies to the smoothing thesis is a documented instance where the state hid, destroyed, sanitized, or too-cleanly denied — converting later pattern-recognition into false intent-certainty. That is Olmsted's mechanism, instanced.
- Every intent/causation edge ("therefore the state was lying / running the plot") is separate, weaker, and attributed to a named claimant. Expect a high exception count: rejected-vs-executed, failure-vs-lie, and destroyed-vs-never-existed are live seams at nearly every anchor. That is the rail working correctly (R-7).

## Key Findings

**The connection-edges are strong and near-fully primary/institutional.** The Church Committee's existence, scope, statistics, and reports; the Northwoods memo's text, signatory, date, and rejection; COINTELPRO's dates, directive language, exposure route, and congressional condemnation; and the NSA's own Tonkin finding are all confirmable in congressional reports, declassified documents (JFK Records Review Board, NSA FOIA releases), court filings, and the National Security Archive's published facsimiles. `real-enemies` and `mkultra` already stand; this harvest supplies the sibling over-tidyings that make the pattern a pattern.

**The intent/causation-edges are separate, weaker, always attributed.** No documented over-tidying licenses its intent-claim to inherit its strength. "Records were destroyed" (flat) never becomes "therefore the alleged plot was real" (unlicensed). Olmsted's frame is descriptive, not exculpatory and not licensing — it explains why smoothing breeds certainty; it validates no specific certainty.

### Anchor 1 — The Church Committee (1975–76): the documentary spine
**Connection [primary/congressional record]:** The **Senate Select Committee to Study Governmental Operations with Respect to Intelligence Activities**, chaired by Senator **Frank Church** (D-ID), was established by S. Res. 21 on **27 January 1975** and issued its final report **29 April 1976**. It investigated abuses by the CIA, FBI, NSA, and IRS. Scale (Senate historical office): **126 full-committee meetings, ~40 subcommittee hearings, ~800 witnesses interviewed, 110,000 documents examined, peak staff of 150, 96 recommendations.** [PRIMARY — US Senate official] https://www.senate.gov/about/powers-procedures/investigations/church-committee.htm ; full citations PDF https://www.senate.gov/about/resources/pdf/church-committee-full-citations.pdf
**Connection [primary] — the assassination-plots interim report:** *Alleged Assassination Plots Involving Foreign Leaders* (interim report, **20 November 1975**) documented CIA plots or plot-support against **Fidel Castro** (the "toxic cigars, exploding seashells, and hypodermic needles disguised as ballpoint pens"), **Patrice Lumumba** (Congo), **Rafael Trujillo** (Dominican Republic), **General René Schneider** (Chile), and the context of **Ngo Dinh Diem** (Vietnam). Report's core finding, verbatim: "The evidence establishes that the United States was implicated in several assassination plots." Committee's assessment: "short of war, assassination is incompatible with American principles, international order, and morality." [PRIMARY — via National Security Archive briefing book, 50th anniversary] https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/briefing-book/intelligence/2025-11-20/cia-assassination-plots-church-committee-report-50-years
**Connection [primary] — the final report's verdict:** The multi-book final report (six books; Book II *Intelligence Activities and the Rights of Americans*, Book III *Supplementary Detailed Staff Reports*, Senate Report No. 94-755, 26 April 1976) concluded that "intelligence agencies have undermined the constitutional rights of citizens" and that "domestic activities of the intelligence community at times violated specific statutory prohibitions and infringed the constitutional rights of American citizens." Executive response: President Ford's Executive Order 11905 (18 Feb 1976): "No employee of the United States Government shall engage in, or conspire to engage in, political assassination."
**The over-tidying [primary — this is the smoothing content]:** The Committee's own record documents that it worked *against* destroyed and withheld files — most sharply the **MKUltra record destruction** (Anchor 5), which it could only partially reconstruct because DCI Richard Helms had ordered the files burned in 1973. The Committee also obtained the CIA's internal **"Family Jewels"** compilation of misconduct. The Church Committee is therefore double-coded on this rail: it is BOTH the documented correction (the state investigating itself) AND the record that proves the prior over-tidying (destroyed files, decades of concealment).
**Intent/causation edge:** The Committee documented *abuses that happened*; it did not license the broader intent-narratives (that the abuses were a single coordinated design). Its own framing was institutional drift — "constitutional checks and balances had not been applied to intelligence operations since the Franklin Roosevelt administration." Keep "the abuses were real and documented" (flat) apart from "therefore [any specific plot theory] is true" (unlicensed).
**Grade-exception (correction vs cover-up seam):** The Church Committee cuts both ways and the node must hold the seam open — it is the strongest documentation of state over-tidying AND the strongest evidence that the system can surface and curb it. Do not resolve to either "proof the state is a conspiracy" or "proof the system works." Record both.

### Anchor 2 — Operation Northwoods (13 March 1962): the model rejected plan
**Connection [primary/declassified]:** A Joint Chiefs of Staff memorandum, **"Justification for US Military Intervention in Cuba (TS)," 13 March 1962**, signed by JCS Chairman General **Lyman L. Lemnitzer** and forwarded to Secretary of Defense **Robert McNamara** as "a preliminary submission for planning purposes." It proposed manufacturing pretexts to justify a US invasion of Cuba, including: staging attacks to "place the United States in the apparent position of suffering defensible grievances from a rash and irresponsible government of Cuba"; a "Remember the Maine" incident — the "real or simulated" sinking of a US ship; **"sinking boats of Cuban refugees on the high seas"** and "possible assassination of Cuban immigrants"; and a faked shootdown of a civilian airliner using a remote-controlled drone "secretly repainted" in the airliner's markings. [PRIMARY — National Security Archive facsimile] https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/CMC-60/joint-chiefs-pretexts-to-invade-Cuba-1962 ; [SECONDARY] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Northwoods
**Connection [primary] — REJECTED, not executed:** President **Kennedy rejected** the plan; per a Lansdale memo of 16 March 1962, Kennedy said "bluntly that we were not discussing the use of military force," and Lemnitzer was subsequently **not reappointed** as JCS Chairman. This is the load-bearing qualifier: Northwoods is "the government really did *propose* X," never "the government did X."
**Connection [primary] — declassification provenance:** Declassified by the **John F. Kennedy Assassination Records Review Board on 18 November 1997**; brought to public attention by James Bamford's *Body of Secrets* (2001); complete document published online by the **National Security Archive, 30 April 2001**. (Search-surface sources also cite a 6 November 1998 CNN *Cold War* series appearance — record the minor provenance-date variance; the ARRB 1997 declassification is the firm anchor.)
**Why it belongs on this rail:** This is **Olmsted's own instance** (see Anchor 0). Its function for the smoothing thesis is precise: a fully documented case that the government *did* draft pretext/false-flag operations proves the pattern is not paranoid *in kind* — which is exactly the accelerant Olmsted names, and exactly why the reader must hold "they proposed it once, and it was rejected" apart from "therefore [later event] was a false flag."
**Intent/causation edge:** That Northwoods proves any *subsequent* event (later shootdowns, later "incidents") was a false flag is the unlicensed leap the rail exists to firewall. Northwoods documents a rejected proposal by named men on a dated memo; it licenses nothing downstream.
**Grade-exception (proposed vs executed):** The single most-abused fact on this rail. The document is real, primary, and damning as a *proposal*; every use of it to certify a later event is attributed conjecture, graded weak/apocryphal. Keep the "rejected" flatly on the record.

### Anchor 3 — COINTELPRO (1956–71): the documented domestic-disruption program
**Connection [primary/FBI documents + congressional record]:** **COINTELPRO** ("Counter Intelligence Program") ran from **1956 to 1971** under FBI Director J. Edgar Hoover — a series of covert operations to surveil, infiltrate, discredit, and disrupt domestic political groups. The program's own directive language, quoted in the exposed files and the Church Committee record: to **"expose, disrupt, misdirect, discredit, or otherwise neutralize"** targeted groups. Documented targets included the Communist Party USA, the Socialist Workers Party, the civil-rights movement (the Southern Christian Leadership Conference and **Martin Luther King Jr.**), the Black Panther Party, the anti-Vietnam-War movement, the American Indian Movement, and Puerto Rican independence groups. [SECONDARY — Wikipedia, quoting FBI directives] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COINTELPRO ; [SECONDARY — Britannica] https://www.britannica.com/topic/COINTELPRO
**Connection [primary] — the exposure route (the anti-smoothing):** COINTELPRO was concealed until **8 March 1971**, when the **Citizens' Commission to Investigate the FBI** burglarized the FBI resident agency in **Media, Pennsylvania**, removed the files, and mailed them to news organizations — which forced the program's existence into the open and helped precipitate the Church Committee. The burglars remained anonymous until self-identifying in 2014. [SECONDARY] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Citizens%27_Commission_to_Investigate_the_FBI
**Connection [primary] — the congressional verdict:** Church Committee **Book III** (Senate Report No. 94-755, 26 April 1976) condemned it verbatim: the Bureau "conducted a sophisticated vigilante operation aimed squarely at preventing the exercise of First Amendment rights of speech and association, on the theory that preventing the growth of dangerous groups and the propagation of dangerous ideas would protect national security." [PRIMARY — Church Committee Book III]
**Intent/causation edge:** The documented program is disruption of lawful political activity; specific downstream intent-claims (e.g., that COINTELPRO orchestrated particular deaths) are separate, attributed, and mostly weak/contested — do not let "the program existed and targeted King" leak into any specific assassination-direction claim without its own primary and its own named claimant.
**Grade-exception (scale framing):** Sources note COINTELPRO was "approximately 0.2% of the FBI's overall workload" — a real datum that cuts against a "the whole Bureau was this" reading. Record it; it neither excuses nor inflates. The documented fact is the program, its directive language, and its exposure.

### Anchor 4 — Gulf of Tonkin (1964): the documented distortion (failure, not proven lie)
**Connection [primary/NSA-declassified]:** In **2005** the NSA declassified an internal historical study by agency historian **Robert J. Hanyok** — *"Skunks, Bogies, Silent Hounds, and the Flying Fish: The Gulf of Tonkin Mystery, 2–4 August 1964"* (originally in the classified *Cryptologic Quarterly*, Winter 2000/Spring 2001). Its finding: a real engagement occurred on **2 August 1964** (North Vietnamese torpedo boats did attack USS *Maddox*, "under questionable circumstances"), but the **4 August "second attack" did not occur**, and the SIGINT was mishandled. [PRIMARY — NSA release, via National Security Archive] https://nsarchive2.gwu.edu/NSAEBB/NSAEBB132/press20051201.htm ; primary study PDF https://www.nsa.gov/portals/75/documents/news-features/declassified-documents/gulf-of-tonkin/articles/release-1/rel1_skunks_bogies.pdf ; [SECONDARY] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Tonkin_incident
**Connection [primary] — the over-tidying, in Hanyok's words:** Hanyok found officials "mishandled" SIGINT and gave leadership "skewed" intelligence; he wrote there was **"an active effort to make SIGINT fit the claim of what happened during the evening of 4 August."** The reporting was selective: **"The overwhelming body of reports, if used, would have told the story that no attack occurred,"** and roughly **90% of the relevant SIGINT that did not support an attack was omitted** from the material sent to policymakers. This is the documented distortion — the sanitized intelligence picture.
**Intent/causation edge [firewalled — this is the key R-3 split]:** Hanyok is explicit that there is **no "smoking gun" proving a deliberate top-level cover-up**. His characterization is a SIGINT-handling failure "skewed" toward a predetermined conclusion — which is NOT the same as a proven Presidential lie. Some secondary sources (and the popular "the Lie That Sparked the War" framing) collapse Hanyok's "skewed intelligence" into "deliberate fabrication by the Johnson administration"; that stronger intent-claim is attributed and contested, not what the NSA study establishes. Grade the documented distortion `documented`; grade "it was a deliberate lie to start a war" as an attributed assertion, weaker.
**Grade-exception (failure vs lie; and the Iraq echo):** The seam to keep open: distortion is documented; deliberateness at the top is not established. Also on the record (relevant to *why* the study stayed classified): per the National Security Archive, government historians' push to release Hanyok was "rebuffed by policy makers concerned that comparisons might be made to intelligence used to justify the Iraq War" — a documented instance of *delayed release* that is itself a small smoothing. Record it; do not build the Iraq comparison into a claim.
