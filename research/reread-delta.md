## The discipline held in this pass (stated so it can be checked)
1. **Surgical, never a rewrite.** Each proposal is AT MOST one added paragraph or one re-aimed sentence. Where an essay would need more than that to come current, the verdict is **NO EDIT — flagged for a separate keystone re-issue**, never a rewrite smuggled in as a "delta."
2. **Touch only what the graph now PROVES.** Every added clause rests on a WELD node already built, with its primary already attached (R-2). No proposal asserts anything past its node's standing. Weight told the essay what it is obliged to *notice*; it licensed no stronger claim (R-12).
3. **Silent discipline (R-6).** Every proposed sentence is written flat and load-bearing, in the house voice — no "the covert state also did this," no "as the corpus now shows," no method-narration. The words below are the actual words, so the operator judges prose, not intent.
4. **Firewalls preserved (R-8).** No proposal states or implies West programmed Manson, that any later incident was a false flag, that Tonkin was a proven top-level lie, or that MKUltra continues. Each carries its own seam in-prose.
5. **Conservative by default.** A genuine under-read is one where the essay's own argument is visibly weaker for not noticing a now-adjacent documented fact. Most essays are not that, and say so.
