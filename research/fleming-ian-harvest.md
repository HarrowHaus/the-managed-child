## The DOCUMENTED CORE — the WELD material

### 1. Naval Intelligence Division career [PRIMARY / documented]
- **Recruited May 1939** by **Rear Admiral John Godfrey, Director of Naval Intelligence**, to be Godfrey's **personal assistant**; joined full-time **August 1939**; codename **"17F."** Worked from **Room 39** at the Admiralty. Commissioned **lieutenant, Royal Naval Volunteer Reserve** (July 1939), promoted **lieutenant-commander** (and nominally commander) despite no prior naval or intelligence training. Fleming served as Godfrey's fixer and liaison to SIS/MI6, SOE, MI5 and other services. [Wikipedia (Ian Fleming); National WWII Museum]
- **The Trout Memo (29 September 1939)** — issued under Godfrey's name but reflecting Fleming's thinking — compared wartime deception to fly-fishing and listed schemes against the Axis. **Item 28** proposed planting misleading papers on a corpse for the enemy to recover; this was later refined into **Operation Mincemeat (1943)**, in which a corpse dressed as a Royal Marine officer, carrying false invasion plans, was floated ashore to fascist-friendly Spain. [Wikipedia (Operation Mincemeat; Ian Fleming); National WWII Museum]
- **30 Assault Unit (30AU)** — Fleming **conceived and directed** this specialist commando intelligence-gathering unit (formed 1942, based partly on a German model), which raced ahead of Allied lines to seize enemy documents, codebooks, and technology; he selected targets and directed from the rear (1942 until reassignment mid-1944). The later **T-Force** (Aug 1944) did the same in liberated territory. [Wikipedia; National WWII Museum]

### 2. The James Bond novels [PRIMARY / documented]
- ***Casino Royale*** — written **15 January–16 February 1952** at **Goldeneye** (his Jamaica estate); **published 13 April 1953** (Jonathan Cape, 10s 6d). **Twelve Bond novels and two short-story collections followed, 1953–1966** (posthumous titles included). Bond's rank (commander, RNVR), background, and the *Goldfinger* commando unit map closely onto Fleming's own service and 30AU. [Wikipedia (Ian Fleming; Casino Royale)]
- **Literary borrowing from the occult milieu (attributed, R-3):** the villain **Le Chiffre** (*Casino Royale*) and other Bond antagonists are often said to draw partly on **Crowley's** persona — a literary-influence claim (the corpus already carries the same "Crowley-modeled villains" note for `wheatley-dennis`). This is a plausible, oft-repeated **attributed** literary tie, not a documented statement of intent; keep it as prose, `possible`.
