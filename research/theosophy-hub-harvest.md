# The Theosophy Hub Rail: A Sourced Connection Harvest

*Harvest for the THEOSOPHY HUB. Produces the evidence base a node-builder grades against; builds no node. Format mirrors `research/crowley-in-america-rail-harvest.md`. Discipline: RULINGS.md R-1 (search before grading), R-2 (evidence gate), R-3 (connection ≠ intent), R-7 (contradictions kept open). MASTER-PLAN Part III §A names the theosophy branch as part of the occult spine; it is already partly built as the four-idiom through-line (Besant / World-Teacher · Bailey / Hierarchy).*

## TL;DR
- The documented spine holds at full strength and is almost entirely institutional-record and primary-text: the **Theosophical Society was founded in New York City, 17 November 1875, by Helena Blavatsky, Henry Steel Olcott, and William Quan Judge** (with sixteen others); it declared **three Objects** (universal brotherhood; comparative study of religion/philosophy/science; investigation of unexplained laws of nature and latent human powers); its founders **relocated the headquarters to Adyar, Madras** (took residence 19 December 1882; Adyar Library founded 1886); Blavatsky wrote **Isis Unveiled (1877)** and **The Secret Doctrine (1888)** and attributed the doctrine to hidden **"Masters"/Mahatmas**; Olcott was **first President** and the **Buddhist Catechism (1881)** author who revived Ceylon Buddhism.
- Three **documented schisms** connect this hub to already-built nodes without any chain-of-succession claim: **Judge's 1895 American-section split** (Boston convention, 28–29 April 1895, vote 191–10; Judge President-for-life; → Point Loma/Tingley); **Rudolf Steiner's departure** (founded the Anthroposophical Society 28 December 1912; expelled from the TS 7 March 1913; over the Krishnamurti/World-Teacher identification); and **Alice Bailey's 1920 break from Besant's Adyar TS** (expelled from the Esoteric Section; → Lucifer Publishing Co. 1922 / Lucis Trust; Arcane School 1923). Each is a *break*, not a lineage — that is the point of building them.
- The **education bridge** is documented and load-bearing for this project: **Maria Montessori was hosted at Adyar (invited by TS President George Arundale and Rukmini Devi Arundale, arrived 1939), interned there as an enemy alien during WWII, and ran ~16 teacher-training courses (1,000+ teachers) from the Adyar estate** — the concrete tie from the theosophy hub into the child/education (enactment) rail.
- Every belief-claim is firewalled: **that the Masters were real adepts steering human evolution is Blavatsky's own attributed doctrine, not documented fact**, and its documented counter-record is the **1885 Hodgson/SPR report** calling her an impostor — which is *itself* contested by the SPR's own **1986 Vernon Harrison reassessment**. Both stay open (R-7). Expect a high exception count; that is the rail working.

## Key Findings

**The connection-edges are strong and near-fully documentable from institutional records and primary texts.** The Society's founding date, place, founders, and three Objects; the move to Adyar; Blavatsky's two major books; Olcott's presidency and Buddhist work; the three schisms; and Montessori's residence at Adyar are all confirmable in the Society's own publications, contemporary reports (SPR *Proceedings*, 1885), and standard scholarship. Besant and Leadbeater already exist as graded nodes; this harvest supplies the hub they hang from and flags the missing anchors.

**The intent/belief-edges are separate, weaker, and always attributed.** The reality of the Masters, the notion that theosophy "steered" internationalism or engineered the child, and even the *fraud* finding against Blavatsky are all interpretive/attributed claims, each firewalled behind its claimant and graded as an assertion — never allowed to inherit the flat strength of the connection.

## Details

### Anchor 1 — The founding (New York, 17 November 1875)
**Connection [primary/institutional]:** The Theosophical Society was formed in New York City on 17 November 1875 by Helena Petrovna Blavatsky, Colonel Henry Steel Olcott, and William Quan Judge, together with sixteen others; Olcott was elected first President and Blavatsky Corresponding Secretary. The Society's own statement of its **Three Objects** (verbatim, TS in America): "(1) To form a nucleus of the universal brotherhood of humanity, without distinction of race, creed, sex, caste or color. (2) To encourage the comparative study of religion, philosophy, and science. (3) To investigate unexplained laws of nature and the powers latent in humanity." [PRIMARY — Theosophical Society official] https://www.theosophical.org/about/about-the-society ; [SECONDARY] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theosophical_Society ; [SECONDARY — Theosophy Wiki] https://www.theosophy.wiki/en/Founding_of_the_Theosophical_Society
**Intent/causation edge:** That the Society was founded to *steer* a coming age or to seed later internationalism is not the founding record — the initial stated aim was "the study and elucidation of Occultism, the Cabala etc." The steering-thesis belongs to the Bailey/Lucis-Trust downstream branch (already firewalled in `bailey-alice`), not to 1875.
**Grade-exception (date):** Sources give the formal inaugural date as **17 November 1875** (adoption of by-laws / Olcott's inaugural address) but the *first organizing meeting* was **September 1875**; "late 1875" is the safe hedge. Record both; do not collapse.

### Anchor 2 — Helena Blavatsky (the doctrine and its documented refutation)
**Connection [primary]:** Blavatsky authored *Isis Unveiled: A Master-Key to the Mysteries of Ancient and Modern Science and Theology* (2 vols., 1877) and *The Secret Doctrine* (2 vols., 1888), the founding expositions of theosophical doctrine; she described theosophy as "the synthesis of science, religion and philosophy." Full text of *The Secret Doctrine* Vol. II online at the Society's own press. [PRIMARY — Theosophical University Press] https://www.theosociety.org/pasadena/sd-pdf/SecretDoctrineVol2_eBook.pdf ; [SECONDARY] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isis_Unveiled ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helena_Blavatsky
**Intent/belief edge [Blavatsky, attributed] — the Masters/Mahatmas:** Blavatsky claimed the doctrines "were not her own invention" but received from a "brotherhood of secretive spiritual adepts whom she referred to as the 'Masters' or 'Mahatmas,'" residing chiefly in Tibet, who "guided humanity's spiritual evolution." This is the theosophical form of the root impulse (an elect administering human development) — but it is **her attributed claim, not documented fact**. Firewall exactly as the Crowley rail firewalls "the Secret Chiefs" (R-3, R-8). [SECONDARY] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helena_Blavatsky
**Connection [primary] — the documented refutation (R-3, catalogue it):** In **December 1885** the Society for Psychical Research (London) published a ~200-page report by **Richard Hodgson** in its *Proceedings*. Hodgson, sent to Adyar (arrived December 1884), concluded — corroborating the housekeeper **Emma Coulomb** — that Blavatsky herself wrote the "Mahatma Letters" in a disguised hand and was "engaged in forgery and deception on an impressive scale." The committee's verdict, verbatim: Blavatsky was "one of the most accomplished, ingenious, and interesting impostors in history." [PRIMARY — SPR *Proceedings* 1885, via] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hodgson_Report ; [SECONDARY — SPR Psi Encyclopedia] https://psi-encyclopedia.spr.ac.uk/articles/hodgson-report-theosophy
**Grade-exception (contradiction kept OPEN — R-7):** The 1885 fraud finding is **itself contested by the SPR's own later reassessment.** In 1986 the SPR *Journal* published handwriting expert **Vernon Harrison**'s analysis calling the Hodgson report "riddled with slanted statements, conjectures advanced as fact... uncorroborated testimony of unnamed witnesses... and downright falsity"; the SPR's own 8 May 1986 press release stated Blavatsky "was unjustly condemned." This is a documented refutation *and* a documented re-refutation. Keep both on the record; pick neither. The node states: Coulomb accused; Hodgson/SPR (1885) found fraud; Harrison/SPR (1986) found the finding unsound. [SECONDARY] https://www.theosophical.org/publications/quest-magazine/h-p-blavatsky-and-the-spr-an-examination-of-the-hodgson-report-of-1885

### Anchor 3 — Henry Steel Olcott (first president; the Buddhist and library work)
**Connection [primary/institutional]:** Colonel Henry Steel Olcott (2 August 1832 – 17 February 1907), American military officer, lawyer and journalist, was **co-founder and first President** of the Society. In Ceylon (Sri Lanka) he formally became a Buddhist, worked to **revive Buddhism**, and wrote the **Buddhist Catechism (1881)**, still in use; the Society founded Buddhist schools including Ananda College (1886), Dharmaraja College (1887), Maliyadeva College (1888) and Mahinda College (1892). He is still honored in Sri Lanka. He also established the **Adyar Library and Research Centre** at the headquarters. [SECONDARY] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_Steel_Olcott ; [PRIMARY — TS Adyar official] https://www.ts-adyar.org/henry-steel-olcott ; [SECONDARY — TS in America] https://www.theosophical.org/component/content/article?id=1819:olcott-and-buddhism
**Intent/causation edge:** Olcott's Buddhist revivalism is a documented program of religious reform, not evidence for any "managed" design over Ceylon; the connection is his organizing and authorship, flat.
**Grade-exception:** none material; dates and titles corroborate across TS-official and third-party sources.

### Anchor 4 — The move to Adyar, Madras (1882 / 1886)
**Connection [primary/institutional]:** Blavatsky and Olcott left New York **17 December 1878**, reached Bombay **16 February 1879** (first Indian HQ), and on **31 May 1882** were shown Huddleston Gardens on the south bank of the Adyar River near Madras; they **took up residence at Adyar on 19 December 1882**, establishing the permanent International Headquarters. The **Adyar Library** was founded **1886**. [SECONDARY] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theosophical_Society_Adyar ; [PRIMARY — TS Adyar official] https://www.ts-adyar.org/early-history
**Grade-exception (date, keep open):** The brief names "1882/1886" — resolve as two distinct documented events, not a conflict to pick: **residence/HQ 19 Dec 1882**; **Adyar Library 1886**. Record both dates with their referents so neither is mistaken for "the move."

### Anchor 5 — The 1895 Judge schism (→ Point Loma / already-adjacent nodes)
**Connection [primary/secondary]:** After Blavatsky's death (1891), **William Quan Judge** (1851–1896), co-founder and head of the American Section, fell into dispute with Olcott and Besant, who charged him with misusing "Mahatma messages" (the 1894 "Judge Case"). At the **Ninth Annual Convention of the American Section, Boston, 28–29 April 1895**, delegates from 89 lodges voted **191 to 10** to declare autonomy from Adyar and reorganize as an independent Theosophical Society in America; **Judge was elected President for life**. Olcott withdrew the charters of the seceding branches. Judge died in New York in 1896; **Katherine Tingley** succeeded him (→ the Point Loma community). [SECONDARY] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Quan_Judge ; [PRIMARY — TS Adyar official] https://www.ts-adyar.org/w-q-judge-1851-1896
**Intent/causation edge [attributed]:** *Whether Judge actually forged or fabricated Mahatma messages* is the contested charge — Besant pressed it; Judge's defenders (Tingley, the Point Loma line) rejected it. The **split (connection) is flat and documented**; the **forgery allegation (intent) is attributed to Besant and denied by Judge**, graded as an assertion. Do not let "there was a schism" leak into "Judge was a forger."
**Grade-exception:** the immediate trigger is variously told (Walter R. Old leaking "Judge Case" papers to the *Westminster Gazette*; Besant renewing charges at the 1894 Adyar convention). Record the mechanism as contested detail; the vote and outcome are firm.

### Anchor 6 — Rudolf Steiner's departure (→ `steiner-rudolf`, Anthroposophy)
**Connection [primary/secondary]:** Steiner (1861–1925), General Secretary of the **German Section** of the TS, **founded the Anthroposophical Society on 28 December 1912** and was **expelled from the Theosophical Society on 7 March 1913**. The break turned on the **identification of Jiddu Krishnamurti as the vehicle for the coming World Teacher** (the Order of the Star in the East, Besant/Leadbeater) and on Steiner's objection to the dilution of Christian elements; Besant induced the General Council to revoke the German Section's charter. Roughly **55 of 69 German lodges (~2,500 members) left with Steiner.** [SECONDARY] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rudolf_Steiner_and_the_Theosophical_Society ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anthroposophical_Society ; [SECONDARY — scholarship] John Paull, "Rudolf Steiner: From Theosophy to Anthroposophy (1902–1913)," https://philarchive.org/rec/PAURSF
**Intent/causation edge:** Steiner's later Waldorf pedagogy (esoteric child-development in an educational idiom) is an adjacency into the enactment/education rail, but *that theosophy "produced" Waldorf as a managed-child program* is unstated and unlicensed; the documented tie is the schism and Steiner's own doctrine.
**Grade-exception (date pairing):** the two dates are complementary, not conflicting — **Anthroposophical Society founded 28 Dec 1912**; **TS expulsion 7 March 1913.** Keep both. `steiner-rudolf` is currently a HYPOTHESIS stub (`incubating: true`); this anchor supplies its confirming record.

### Anchor 7 — Alice Bailey's break (→ `bailey-alice`, `lucis-trust`)
**Connection [primary/secondary]:** At the TS annual convention (Chicago, **1920**) a power struggle between forces loyal to Besant/the Esoteric Section and their critics broke into the open; **Alice Bailey** objected to Besant's "neo-Theosophy" and to required pledges of loyalty, and Besant questioned Bailey's claimed communications with "the Tibetan" (Djwal Khul). **Alice and Foster Bailey were expelled**; they went on to incorporate the **Lucifer Publishing Company (1922)** — later **Lucis Trust** — and to found the **Arcane School (1923)**. [SECONDARY] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alice_Bailey ; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annie_Besant
**Intent/belief edge [Bailey, attributed]:** Bailey's doctrine of an **initiated Hierarchy guiding humanity toward a planned unity** is the third of the project's four idioms — already firewalled in `bailey-alice` as *testimony to the impulse, held apart from the sociology as cause*. The downstream "theosophy → internationalism / UN meditation room" pipeline (Lucis Trust) is a separate documented thread, kept behind its own attribution.
**Grade-exception:** already handled in the built node (the "Lucifer Publishing" name is a documented fact and a conspiracy favorite; it denotes the Theosophical "light-bearer" usage and is **not** evidence of a cabal). Carry that caveat up to the hub. This anchor confirms **the break was a schism, not a succession** (matches `bailey-alice` `broke-from` edge).

### Anchor 8 — The Krishnamurti hinge (already built; hub adjacency)
**Connection [secondary]:** Leadbeater identified Krishnamurti at Adyar in 1909; Besant founded the **Order of the Star in the East (April 1911)**; Krishnamurti **dissolved it himself at Ommen, 3 August 1929**. These are built as `krishnamurti-jiddu`, `order-of-the-star`, `besant-annie`, `leadbeater-cw`. The hub's job is to be the *institution* those articulation-nodes descend into; the Krishnamurti episode is also the documented cause of both the Steiner (Anchor 6) and (contextually) the Bailey (Anchor 7) departures — one event, two schisms, held apart. [SECONDARY] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Order_of_the_Star_in_the_East
**Note:** no new intent-claim here beyond what the built nodes already firewall (Besant "groomed" Krishnamurti — in-vocabulary, documented, never softened to "mentored").

### Anchor 9 — The education bridge: Maria Montessori at Adyar (theosophy → the child)
**Connection [primary/secondary]:** **Maria Montessori** (invited by TS International President **George Arundale** and his wife **Rukmini Devi Arundale**, who visited her in Holland) arrived in **India in 1939, aged 69, and made Adyar her home** with her son Mario. When Italy entered WWII (1940), the British authorities **interned mother and son as enemy aliens**; Maria was restricted by **house arrest to the Adyar Theosophical Society estate** (Mario briefly interned at Ahmednagar; the Viceroy reunited them as a 70th-birthday gift on **31 August 1940**). Despite internment she was permitted to teach: she and Mario ran **~16 residential teacher-training courses (Adyar, Kodaikanal, Ahmedabad, Karachi), training 1,000+ teachers** (one Adyar course drew ~300). The Society sponsored the work; her "Cosmic Education" was received as consonant with Theosophical ideas of "the evolution and the oneness of life." [SECONDARY — Wikipedia] https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montessori_in_India ; [SECONDARY/PRIMARY — TS in America, *Quest*] https://www.theosophical.org/publications/quest-magazine/montessori-and-the-theosophical-society ; [PRIMARY — AMI Montessori Archives, 1939 arrival Adyar] https://archives.montessori-ami.org/do/9a07fe6e-429c-4c3d-94f8-df0733bee212
**Intent/causation edge [attributed, FIREWALLED]:** *That theosophy engineered, steered, or ideologically captured Montessori pedagogy as a "managed child" instrument* is **not documented** — the record shows a documented **host relationship** (invitation, residence, sponsorship, doctrinal affinity), not a program transfer. The connection carries the bridge into the enactment rail; the intent stays behind whoever asserts it. State the tie as "hosted, interned at, and trained teachers from the Adyar TS estate," never as "theosophy produced Montessori education."
**Grade-exception (dates, keep open — R-7):** her departure/return is given inconsistently across sources — she left India **1946**, or "returned to Europe **1947** and came back the same year," with a final departure often dated **1949**. Record the range (1939 arrival; war years at Adyar; c. 1946–1949 departures/returns); do not pick a single exit date.

## Recommendations
1. **Build the hub on the connection-edges only.** Founding (1875, three Objects), Adyar move (1882/1886), the two books, Olcott's presidency/Buddhist work, and the three schisms are all primary/institutional and can be stated flat and unhedged.
2. **Firewall every belief-edge behind its claimant and grade it as an assertion.** "Blavatsky claimed the Masters imparted the doctrine" (attributed) ≠ "the Masters guided evolution" (never stated as fact). Same discipline the Crowley rail applies to the Secret Chiefs.
3. **Treat the schisms as breaks, not a chain (the anti-corkboard move).** Judge, Steiner, and Bailey each *left*; the honest edge is `broke-from` / departure, not `succeeded`. This is what keeps the hub from manufacturing a false lineage.
4. **Carry the Hodgson↔Harrison contradiction into the node unresolved (R-7).** The fraud finding and its 1986 refutation both stand on the record; the node marks the seam and picks neither.
5. **The Montessori bridge is the payoff for this project specifically** — the one documented edge that carries theosophy out of the occult spine and into the child/education (enactment) rail. Build it as a *host* relationship, intent firewalled.
6. **Benchmarks that would change grades:** locating the original SPR *Proceedings* (Vol. III, 1885) text would upgrade the Hodgson quote from secondary-relayed to primary-verified; primary Montessori/Arundale correspondence would let the education-bridge edge state the terms of the invitation precisely; Judge-case primary papers would let the forgery *allegation* be graded on its own evidence rather than by attribution alone.

## Caveats
- **Contradictions kept open, not resolved (R-7):** founding date (Sept 1875 first meeting vs 17 Nov 1875 formal); Adyar "1882 residence" vs "1886 library"; the Blavatsky fraud finding (Hodgson/SPR 1885) vs its unsoundness finding (Harrison/SPR 1986); Montessori's departure/return (1946 / 1947 / 1949); the trigger mechanism of the Judge case. All recorded, none picked.
- **Attributed, not endorsed:** the Masters/Mahatmas doctrine (Blavatsky's own claim); the Judge-forgery charge (Besant's, denied by Judge); any "theosophy steered internationalism/education" reading (belongs to the Bailey/Lucis downstream, already firewalled).
- **Source-tier honesty:** the load-bearing facts rest on the Society's own publications (TS in America, TS Adyar official, Theosophical University Press full texts), the SPR *Proceedings*/Psi Encyclopedia, the AMI Montessori Archives, and named scholarship (John Paull on Steiner; Vernon Harrison on the Hodgson report); Wikipedia and Theosophy Wiki were used for orientation and corroboration, not as sole authority. Grokipedia appeared in results and was **not** relied on for any load-bearing claim.
- **The deliverable is a picture, not a proof.** The institutional record is dense and real; the belief that would make theosophy a single directed program steering the child is exactly what is *not* documented. That gap is the finding.
