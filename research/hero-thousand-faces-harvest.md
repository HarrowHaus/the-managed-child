## TL;DR

## The PRIMARY — *The Hero with a Thousand Faces* (Campbell, 1949)

**Real title:** *The Hero with a Thousand Faces.*
**Author:** Joseph Campbell (1904–1987).
**First edition:** **1949**, **Bollingen Foundation** / **Pantheon Books**, **Bollingen Series XVII** (the seventeenth title in the series). The Bollingen Series was funded by the **Mellon family** (Paul and Mary Mellon's Bollingen Foundation) — the publication context the harvest was asked to note: the monomyth entered print under elite philanthropic patronage, alongside Jung and the Eranos circle. (Later reissues: Princeton/Bollingen, 1968 2nd ed.; New World Library, 2008 3rd ed.)
**The claim it makes:** all the world's hero-myths are surface variants of a single deep structure (the **monomyth**), decomposable into **separation → initiation → return** across **seventeen stages** (e.g., Call to Adventure, Refusal of the Call, Supernatural Aid, Crossing the First Threshold, Belly of the Whale, Road of Trials, Apotheosis, The Ultimate Boon, Master of Two Worlds, Freedom to Live).
**Provenance of the word:** Campbell **borrowed "monomyth" from James Joyce's *Finnegans Wake*** (1939).

### Verbatim lines that state the template [PRIMARY — text, cross-checked Wikipedia + Joseph Campbell Foundation]
*(quoted short, per fair-use)*

- The monomyth, in one sentence: **"A hero ventures forth from the world of common day into a region of supernatural wonder: fabulous forces are there encountered and a decisive victory is won: the hero comes back from this mysterious adventure with the power to bestow boons on his fellow man."** (~p.30, 1949 Bollingen ed.; ~p.23 later eds.)
- The three-act skeleton / the reusable unit: **"The standard path of the mythological adventure of the hero is a magnification of the formula represented in the rites of passage: separation — initiation — return: which might be named the nuclear unit of the monomyth."** (same location.)
- The word's source, in Campbell's own gloss: he attributes **"monomyth"** to Joyce's *Finnegans Wake*.

**Note on the page number (L2 precision):** the canonical passage is cited as **p.30** in the original 1949 Bollingen printing and as **p.23** in later resettings (New World Library). Cite both so the primary attaches cleanly whichever edition the node-builder holds.

## The industrialization — Vogler's transmission (the DP.1 spine)

**The 1985 memo [documented].** **Christopher Vogler**, then a story analyst / development executive at **Walt Disney**, wrote a **seven-page internal memo, *A Practical Guide to The Hero with a Thousand Faces*, in 1985**, condensing Campbell's seventeen stages into a working checklist for screen story. Vogler himself calls it **"the legendary seven-pager."**
**Its circulation [documented].** The memo passed hand-to-hand at Disney and then beyond: *"executives at other studios were giving the pamphlet to writers, directors, and producers as guides to universal, commercial story patterns."* It acquired underground fame in Hollywood development culture.
***The Writer's Journey* [documented].** Vogler expanded the memo into **_The Writer's Journey: Mythic Structure for Storytellers and Screenwriters_ (Michael Wiese Productions, 1992; rev. 2nd ed. 1998; 3rd ed. 2007, ISBN 978-1-932907-36-0)** — the book that made the model the de-facto industry template, reducing Campbell's **17 stages to 12**.
***The Lion King* [documented — the one on-record film credit].** Vogler **contributed story material to Disney's *The Lion King*** (per his Wikipedia biography and the trade record); one account frames the studio's turn to him directly: *"when Disney was looking for a formula … Vogler was called in."* This is the cleanest single instance of the template moving from memo to product with a named agent.

### Documented adoption vs. myth-of-influence [R-3 split]
- **DOCUMENTED (Layer 1):** Vogler authored the memo (1985) and *The Writer's Journey* (1992); the memo circulated at Disney and other studios; Vogler is credited with story work on *The Lion King*.
- **DOCUMENTED but via the filmmaker, not via Vogler (Layer 1, `campbell-joseph`):** **George Lucas** publicly credited Campbell — after *Star Wars* (1977) Lucas said the story was shaped in part by *The Hero with a Thousand Faces*, and the **1988 *The Power of Myth* (PBS, Moyers) was filmed at Lucas's Skywalker Ranch**, with Campbell praising *Star Wars* as having "a valid mythological perspective." Keep this precise: **Lucas consciously used Campbell** is documented (Lucas's own statements + the friendship + the venue); it predates Vogler and is a separate transmission from the Vogler/studio one.
- **ATTRIBUTED / JOURNALISTIC (held DOWN — `possible`/interpretive):** that *The Matrix*, *Fight Club*, *Titanic*, *Aladdin*, *Beauty and the Beast*, and the general run of blockbusters "consciously used" Vogler's template. These are critics' pattern-matches; no documented adoption located. Report as the *myth of influence*, not as adoption.

## Campbell's own sources — common-inheritance, NOT the DP.1 spine (Layer 2)

The monomyth is a synthesis; its ancestors are documented and matter only as background depth, never as the thesis's transmission line:
- **Adolf Bastian — "elementary ideas" (*Elementargedanken*)**: the innate cross-cultural motifs vs. their "folk"/local expressions. Campbell's "elementary vs. folk ideas" is Bastian's distinction. [common-inheritance]
- **Carl Jung — archetypes / collective unconscious**: the psychological engine Campbell borrows to explain why the elementary ideas recur. [common-inheritance / network-exposure — Campbell moved in the Bollingen/Eranos-Jung orbit]
- **Arnold van Gennep — *The Rites of Passage* (1909)**: the literal **separation–transition–incorporation** scheme Campbell renames **separation–initiation–return**. The nearest single source of the three-act unit. [common-inheritance]
- **James Frazer — *The Golden Bough***; **Leo Frobenius** (the "belly of the whale" descent/renewal motif, *Das Zeitalter des Sonnengottes*, 1904); **Otto Rank — *The Myth of the Birth of the Hero***. [common-inheritance]

These are Layer-2 ties to be wired (if at all) as `common-inheritance`; they are NOT the DP.1 argument, which is about the template's downstream *industrialization*, not its upstream sources.
