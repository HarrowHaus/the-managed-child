## The PRIMARIES

*(Quoted short, per fair-use. Willard R. Trask is the standard English translator across the corpus.)*

### 1. *The Sacred and the Profane: The Nature of Religion* — the sacred/profane thesis
**Real title:** *The Sacred and the Profane: The Nature of Religion.* **Origin:** German *Das Heilige und das Profane* (**1957**); English trans. **Willard R. Trask, Harcourt, Brace & World, 1959** *(note: Harcourt, not Bollingen)*.
**Verbatim [text cross-checked against the archive.org full text]:**
- *"Man becomes aware of the sacred because it manifests itself, shows itself, as something wholly different from the profane."*
- *"The reader will very soon realize that sacred and profane are two modes of being in the world, two existential situations assumed by man in the course of his history."*
- **Hierophany:** *"…it expresses no more than is implicit in its etymological content, i.e., that something sacred shows itself to us."*
- **Homo religiosus:** *"The man of the traditional societies is admittedly a homo religiosus…"*; and *"…all these facts arise from a single type of behavior, that of homo religiosus."*

### 2. *The Myth of the Eternal Return* — archetypes, repetition, the terror of history
**Real title:** *The Myth of the Eternal Return* (subtitle *Archetypes and Repetition*; U.S. also as *Cosmos and History*). **Origin:** French *Le Mythe de l'éternel retour* (Gallimard, **1949**); English trans. **Willard R. Trask, Bollingen Series XLVI, Pantheon, 1954.**
**The claim it makes:** archaic humanity held that *nothing is real, nothing truly exists unless it participates in a celestial archetype*; ritual **repetition** re-enters "the Great Time" and **abolishes profane time/history**; ch. IV, **"The Terror of History,"** frames myth/repetition as the archaic *technology for defeating history's terror* (against which, Eliade argues, modern historicist man is defenseless). This is the archetype/eternal-return core that rhymes with Jung's archetypes and Campbell's monomyth — a Bollingen-Series sibling.

### 3. *Shamanism: Archaic Techniques of Ecstasy* — the standard universalizing monograph
**Real title:** *Shamanism: Archaic Techniques of Ecstasy.* **Origin:** French *Le Chamanisme et les techniques archaïques de l'extase* (Payot, **1951**); English trans. **Willard R. Trask, Bollingen Series LXXVI (76), Princeton/Bollingen, 1964.** Became **the standard cross-cultural work on shamanism** — the model instance of Eliade's method: gathering worldwide phenomena under one archaic-technique category ("ecstasy," "archaic techniques"), the universalizing move his critics target.

### 4. *Patterns in Comparative Religion* — the systematic statement
**Real title:** *Patterns in Comparative Religion.* **Origin:** French *Traité d'histoire des religions* (Payot, **1949**); English trans. **Rosemary Sheed, 1958.** Eliade's systematic morphology of the sacred (hierophanies organized by sky, water, earth, stones, time, etc.) — the fullest statement of the "the sacred manifests through hierophanies" thesis.

## The network relay — Eranos → Bollingen → Chicago (the DP.2 machinery; WELD)

- **Eranos (Ascona) [documented].** Eliade began lecturing at **Carl Jung's Eranos conferences at Ascona, Switzerland, in 1950** and continued as a regular. Steven Wasserstrom's ***Religion after Religion: Gershom Scholem, Mircea Eliade, and Henry Corbin at Eranos*** (Princeton, 1999) documents that the three "legendary" mid-century scholars of Judaism, history-of-religions, and Islam **all lectured to the Eranos circle between 1949 and 1978**, and jointly "de-emphasized law, ritual, and social history" in favor of "myth and mysticism." Eranos is the shared **network-exposure** milieu binding Eliade, Jung, and (via the Bollingen Eranos Yearbooks) Campbell.
- **Bollingen (the Mellon apparatus) [documented].** A **Bollingen Foundation research grant in 1951** (Paul & Mary Mellon's foundation — the same patronage that published Campbell's *Hero with a Thousand Faces*, Bollingen Series XVII, 1949) relieved Eliade's post-1945 Paris poverty. **Several major works entered the Bollingen Series** (*Myth of the Eternal Return*, XLVI; *Shamanism*, LXXVI). **Cross-node network fact:** **Joseph Campbell edited *Papers from the Eranos Yearbooks* (Bollingen Series XXX)** — the volumes in which Eranos lectures (Eliade's among the circle's) reached English print. This is the documented hinge tying `campbell-joseph` and `eliade-mircea` through the *same* Eranos/Bollingen apparatus — an `institutional-relay` / `network-exposure`, not a person-to-person transmission.
- **Chicago [documented].** In **1957** Eliade joined the **University of Chicago Divinity School**, invited by **Joachim Wach**; on Wach's death he succeeded him and became the **Sewell L. Avery Distinguished Service Professor of the History of Religions (1957–1986)**. From that chair the "Eliadean" / "Chicago school" method became **the dominant paradigm in American history-of-religions** — the export mechanism that made archetypal/mythic thinking academically prestigious in the U.S. (Source: University of Chicago Library, *Guide to the Mircea Eliade Papers 1926–1998*; Encyclopedia.com; New World Encyclopedia.)

## The charged material, handled honestly (R-3 / R-8 — the load-bearing firewall; documented, dated, a real seam)

**The connection [DOCUMENTED, dated — flat].** In the **mid-1930s** the young Eliade (b. 1907) aligned with the **Legion of the Archangel Michael / Iron Guard (Legionary Movement)**, a Romanian ultranationalist, Orthodox-mystical, antisemitic movement under **Corneliu Zelea Codreanu**. He was a disciple of the Legionary-aligned philosopher **Nae Ionescu**; he published in the sympathetic press (**_Vremea_, _Buna Vestire_, _Cuvântul_**); in **1937** he wrote an essay endorsing the Legion / praising Codreanu as a regenerative spiritual leader (widely cited as *"Why I Believe in the Victory of the Legionary Movement,"* *Buna Vestire*, 1937). His **last pro-Legion article appeared February 1938**. Codreanu was **arrested April 1938, executed November 1938.** After WWII, Eliade was **consistently silent/evasive** about this record for the rest of his life. **Standing: documented and dated.** (Sources: Adriana Berger; Leon Volovici, *Nationalist Ideology and Antisemitism*; Daniel Dubuisson; Ivan Strenski; Steven Wasserstrom; Bruce Lincoln; corroborated in the Wikipedia/New World Encyclopedia summaries.)

**The intent / degree / infection-of-the-scholarship [CONTESTED — attributed, split off per R-3/R-8].** What is **contested**, and must be quarantined behind its named claimants, is: (a) the **degree** of complicity — critics (Berger, Dubuisson) push toward deep and lasting antisemitism traceable *through his scholarship*; defenders (**Mac Linscott Ricketts**, **Bryan Rennie**) hold he was a cultural sympathizer, never a formal member or a participant in violence, and that the mature scholarship is separable; and (b) the **claim that "the sacred" itself encodes a crypto-fascist ideology** — the **McCutcheon / Dubuisson** thesis that Eliade's *sui generis*, ahistorical religion is politically loaded. **R-8 firewall:** these are **assertions attributed to named scholars**, graded as assertions, never stated as settled fact; the honest resolution is to **keep the tension and mark the seam (R-7)** — the documented sympathy is real and dated; the reach of its stain on the work is genuinely disputed. **Never launder** (do not soften the 1937 Codreanu praise into "youthful nationalism") and **never inflate** (do not make the node a fascist exposé — the scholarship, and its place in the Eranos/Bollingen machinery, is the thesis-relevant part).
