# The Crowley-in-America Rail: A Sourced Connection Harvest

## TL;DR
- The documented spine holds at full strength: Crowley → OTO/Agape Lodge → Jack Parsons (JPL/Aerojet co-founder) → L. Ron Hubbard → Babalon Working → Kenneth Anger → Process Church → the contested Manson/Son-of-Sam tail is a chain of REAL, citable connection-edges — people who met, read each other, funded, founded, and initiated one another, most confirmable in primary documents (Crowley–Parsons correspondence, Parsons's *Book of Babalon*, Anger's own interviews, Process publications, court filings).
- Every intent/causation edge ("X therefore directed/programmed Y") is separate and weaker, and must be attributed: the Scientology-from-Crowley thesis is Jon Atack's / John Symonds's (courts and most scholars, e.g. Hugh Urban and J. Gordon Melton, reject it as core); the Manson–Process link is Ed Sanders's and Maury Terry's (retracted under libel settlement in the US, rejected in UK court); the modern internet synthesis is Dana / Rotting Jewels's.
- Expect and welcome a high exception count: primary sources are missing or contested at nearly every intent-hop; locations and dates conflict; and multiple sources conflate a documented connection with an unproven intent. This is the rail working correctly.

## Key Findings

**The connection-edges are strong and largely primary-documentable.** Parsons really did co-found JPL and Aerojet AND lead the Agape Lodge; he really did address Crowley as "Most Beloved Father"; Hubbard really did live in his house and form Allied Enterprises; Parsons really did write the *Book of Babalon* with Hubbard as scribe; Anger really was a Crowley devotee who knew Marjorie Cameron and worked with the Rolling Stones and Bobby Beausoleil; the Process really did splinter from Scientology; [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Process_Church_of_the_Final_Judgement) Sanders and Terry really did assemble the Manson–Process case; the Process really did sue and force a retraction.

**The intent-edges are weak, contested, or unprovable, and always belong to a named claimant.** No documented edge licenses its intent-claim to inherit its strength.

## Details

### Anchor 0 — The ideological core (the "old conviction")
**Connection/Documentation [primary]:** Crowley's *The Book of the Law* (Liber AL vel Legis, 1904) proclaims a "New Aeon," the Aeon of Horus, and casts Crowley as its prophet. Verbatim (Liber AL I:15): "Now ye shall know that the chosen priest & apostle of infinite space is the prince-priest the Beast; and in his woman called the Scarlet Woman is all power given." [Archive](https://ia801405.us.archive.org/35/items/CrowleyTheBookOfTheLaw/Crowley,%20Aleister%20-%201904%20-%20The%20Book%20of%20the%20Law.pdf) The E.G.C. Manifesto approved by Crowley: "The world has entered (March, 1904) the New Aeon, the Age of the Crowned and Conquering Child... the Masters have decided that the time has come for the administration of the Sacraments of the Aeon of Horus to those capable of comprehension." [Hermetic Library](https://hermetic.com/dionysos/beyond3)
**Intent/causation edge:** The notion of "an initiated elect steering society toward a planned age" is Crowley's own self-understanding [primary] — he wrote in *Magick* of "an Intelligence praeterhuman and articulate, purposefully interfering in the philosophy, religion, ethics, economics and politics of the Planet." [Thelemapedia](http://www.thelemapedia.org/index.php/Aleister_Crowley) That Crowley's elect ACTUALLY steered anything is not documented; it is the interpretive throughline this whole rail exists to test.
**Grade-exception:** "Secret chiefs" / "the Masters" are Crowley's own theological furniture, not a documented organization. Any leap from Crowley's belief to real-world causation is unlicensed.

### Anchor 1 — Crowley → OTO / Agape Lodge (Pasadena)
**Connection [primary/web-secondary]:** Crowley was chartered into OTO by Theodor Reuss (April 21, 1912) and elected Outer Head of the Order in 1925. Agapé Lodge No. 2 was founded 1935 by Wilfred Talbot Smith in Hollywood (1746 Winona Boulevard); relocated to Pasadena. Crowley appointed Smith X° for the USA. Source (OTO US Grand Lodge official history): "In the 1930s, Wilfred Talbot Smith (1885-1957)... moved from Vancouver on instructions from Crowley to work with Jane Wolfe... to establish Agapé Lodge No. 2 in Los Angeles."
**Connection [primary]:** Crowley's own correspondence drove lodge governance. Wikipedia-corroborated: "In 1942, Aleister Crowley, critical of Wilfred Talbot Smith's management of the Agape Lodge, instructed the replacement of Smith with Jack Parsons as its leader." A March 3, 1945 Crowley letter (in Kenneth Grant's hand) to Parsons demanded members cease contact with Smith "or see the Lodge put under 'interdict'" (Weiser Antiquarian catalog description of the manuscript).
**Grade-exception (conflicting dates/locations):** Sources disagree on the founding sequence — the OTO official history dates the first weekly Gnostic Mass to "Sunday, March 19, 1933," while stating "Agapé Lodge No. 2 held its first meeting in 1935." Wikipedia says founded 1935. Crowley visited LA only once, so the lodge ran by correspondence.

### Anchor 2 — Agape Lodge → Jack Parsons
**Connection [primary/web-secondary]:** Parsons and wife Helen were introduced to the OTO in January 1939 (taken to the Church of Thelema on Winona Blvd by John and Frances Baxter), initiated February 15, 1941, and Parsons became lodge head in 1942 at Crowley's instruction. Parsons addressed Crowley as "Most Beloved Father" and signed "Thy son, John" (*Bare-Faced Messiah* / OTO archives).
**Connection [primary]:** Crowley on Parsons, to Smith: Parsons "is the most valued member of the whole Order, with no exception!" Jane Wolfe to Germer: Parsons was "an A1 man... Crowleyesque in attainment as a matter of fact."
**Connection [primary]:** Parsons funded Crowley — "gave almost all of his salary to the O.T.O.... and financially supported Crowley in London through Germer" (Wikipedia, citing biographies).
**Intent/causation edge:** That Crowley "programmed" or directed Parsons as an American vessel is not supported; the documented relationship is financial patronage + mentorship + disagreement. Crowley criticized Parsons's freelancing (witchcraft "beyond his grade").
**Grade-exception:** Parsons's motto is given inconsistently: "Thelema Obtentum Prodeumque Delta" (Wikipedia), "Thelema Obtentum Procedero Amoris Nuptiae" (Pendle, per Pasadena Now) — both yielding 210. Record the conflict.

### Anchor 3 — Parsons → JPL / Aerojet (rocketry + occultism in one figure)
**Connection [primary/institutional]:** Parsons, with Edward Forman and Frank Malina under Theodore von Kármán, formed the Caltech GALCIT Rocket Research Group (1934); this became JPL (1943); they founded Aerojet (1942). Parsons invented the first castable composite solid rocket propellant. Encyclopædia Britannica (René Ostberg) states Parsons "was central to developing a prototype of a jet-assisted takeoff rocket," that in 1941 "the group founded the Aerojet General Corporation," and in 1944 the Arroyo Seco working space "became the JPL, which is now overseen by NASA" — confirming his co-founder status of both institutions. The International Astronomical Union named a far-side lunar crater "Parsons" in 1972: a 40-km-diameter impact crater at 37.3°N, 171.2°W, west-northwest of Krylov and east of Moore, named "after the rocket engineer and occultist Jack Parsons" (Wikipedia, "Parsons (crater)").
**Connection [primary/web-secondary]:** Parsons fused the two worlds openly. Britannica (Ostberg) records that von Kármán praised Parsons for his "rich talent for chemistry" but called him "a delightful screwball" who "loved to recite pagan poetry to the sky while stamping his feet" (quoted via John Carter, *Sex and Rockets*); Aerojet co-founder Fritz Zwicky said Parsons invoked Pan (reciting Crowley's "Hymn to Pan") before each rocket test. The JPL/Devil's Gate detail: "The location of JPL, near the site of Devil's Gate Dam in Pasadena, was supposedly selected by Parsons due to his belief that the location was a portal to Hell" (Wikipedia — note the hedge "supposedly").
**Connection [primary]:** He was forced out of Aerojet/JPL in 1944 — "Aerojet's Caltech-linked employees... would only agree to the sale on the condition that Parsons and Forman were removed from the company, viewing their occult activities as disreputable" (Wikipedia).
**Grade-exception:** Popular sources overstate ("NASA's founder") — NASA didn't exist until 1958; the accurate claim is co-founder of JPL/Aerojet whose propellant work was foundational. Several outlets (davidmasters Substack) flag this correction themselves.

### Anchor 4 — Parsons → L. Ron Hubbard
**Connection [primary]:** Hubbard moved into the Parsonage in August 1945. Parsons to Crowley: "About three months ago I met Captain L. Ron Hubbard, a writer and explorer of whom I had known for some time... He is a gentleman; he has red hair, green eyes, is honest and intelligent, and we have become great friends. He moved in with me about two months ago, and although Betty and I are still friendly, she has transferred her sexual affection to Ron."
**Connection [primary]:** They formed Allied Enterprises; Hubbard and Sara Northrup took Parsons's money (figures vary — see exception) and boats. Crowley's reaction [primary]: "Suspect Ron playing confidence trick," and "It seems to me on the information of our brethren in California that Parsons has got an illumination in which he has lost all his personal independence... Apparently it is the ordinary confidence trick" (OTO archives, quoted in *Bare-Faced Messiah*).
**Intent/causation edge (Scientology-from-Crowley) — MULTIPLE NAMED CLAIMANTS, graded separately:**
- **[primary]** In the December 1952 Philadelphia Doctorate Course (tape #18, 5212C05), Hubbard called Crowley "the late Aleister Crowley, my very good friend" and recommended *The Master Therion* (Crowley's *Magick in Theory and Practice*) as "the only modern work that has anything to do with them [the magic cults]." [Carnegie Mellon University](https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~dst/Library/Shelf/lamont/guru.htm) Hubbard never met Crowley in person (Wikipedia, "Scientology and the occult," flags this).
- **[primary]** Ronald DeWolf (Hubbard's son, aka Nibs) in a 1983 *Penthouse* interview: "Black magic is the inner core of Scientology," and "my father did not worship Satan. He thought he was Satan." [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scientology_and_the_occult) DeWolf is an unreliable/hostile witness who retracted other allegations — flag.
- **[web-secondary / Jon Atack]** Atack argues Crowley's magic "is at the core of Scientology" (parallels: numbered secret grades, "thetan"/will, 0-based numbering).
- **[web-secondary / scholarly, contrary]** Hugh B. Urban, "The Occult Roots of Scientology?: L. Ron Hubbard, Aleister Crowley, and the Origins of a Controversial New Religion," *Nova Religio* 15, no. 3 (Feb 1, 2012): 91–116 (UC Press), verbatim: "Crowley's occult ideas, I argue, do in fact represent one—but only one—element in the rich, eclectic bricolage that became the early Church of Scientology; but these occult elements are also mixed together with themes drawn from Eastern religions, science fiction, pop psychology, and Hubbard's own fertile imagination." J. Gordon Melton dismisses the connection as core. Massimo Introvigne (CESNUR) notes Hubbard may have been simultaneously curious about Crowley AND informing US intelligence about Parsons. [Bitter Winter](https://bitterwinter.org/gnosticism-dark-legends-on-scientology-founder-discussed-by-scholars/)
**Grade-exception (money figure conflict):** The sum Hubbard took is given as $10,000 (Frieze), "over $20,000" (Grokipedia), and "nearly $21,000" (Remnant). Record all; do not resolve.
**Grade-exception (Scientology's own counter-narrative):** The Church claims Hubbard "was working as an undercover policeman for the LAPD when he infiltrated a black-magic ring." This is the Church's own attributed claim, uncorroborated.

### Anchor 5 — The Babalon Working (1946)
**Connection [primary]:** Documented ritual series, Jan–Mar 1946, Parsons with Hubbard as scribe/seer, drawing on Crowley's sex magick and the "moonchild" concept from Crowley's 1917 novel *Moonchild*. Parsons's own *Book of Babalon* (Liber 49) is the primary text; verbatim opening of his account: "In January 1946 I had been engaged in the study and practice of Magick for seven years, and in the supervision and operation of an occult lodge for four years, having been initiated into the Sanctuary of the Gnosis by the Beast 666, Fra. 132, and Fra. Saturnus." [Hermetic Library](https://hermetic.com/parsons/the-book-of-babalon)
**Connection [primary]:** Liber 49 text itself: "Yea, it is I, BABALON. And this is my book, that is the fourth chapter of the Book of the Law." [Wordpress](https://babalon156.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/book-of-babalon1.pdf) Parsons declared himself Antichrist "Belarion."
**Connection [primary]:** Crowley's contemporaneous disdain, to Germer: "fairly frantic when I contemplate the idiocy of these louts!" [Occult Encyclopedia](https://www.occult.live/index.php/Babalon_Working)
**Intent/causation edge:** Whether the working "did" anything (summoned Babalon, produced consequences) is Parsons's own belief [primary], not documented fact. Marjorie Cameron's arrival Parsons read as the elemental [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Babalon) — his interpretation.
**Grade-exception (date conflict):** The reception date of Liber 49 is given as February 27, February 29, and "Feb 28... commanded to write" across sources (rahoorkhuit.org "February 29, 1946"; hermetic.com "Feb. 28... invoking BABALON"; the working framed "January 4 – March 4, 1946 E.V."). Verses 5–8 are "missing and presumed lost." [Rahoorkhuit](https://www.rahoorkhuit.org/2018/09/the-book-of-babalon.html?m=1) Record all.

### Anchor 6 — Crowley/Parsons orbit → Kenneth Anger
**Connection [primary]:** Anger was a self-identified Thelemite/OTO member and Crowley devotee. In his own words (AnOther interview): "I was introduced to the works of Aleister Crowley by Marjorie Cameron, a very good friend who was also, without any doubt, a genuine witch... She was married to Jack Parsons... After his death in 1952, Marjorie and I lived together and studied the Crowleyian teachings." [AnOther](https://www.anothermag.com/design-living/14890/cult-filmmaker-kenneth-anger-on-his-relationship-with-the-occult) This is the documented hinge connecting the Parsons orbit to Anger.
**Connection [primary]:** Anger (S8 interview): "I'm a member of the OTO – Ordo Templi Orientis – an occult order founded by British genius Aleister Crowley... Lucifer Rising was about Egyptian gods summoning the angel Lucifer – in order to usher in a new occult age, in accordance with the principles of OTO." [(S8)](https://s8cinema.com/en/2024/05/31/kenneth-anger-how-i-made-lucifer-rising/) To Roger Ebert: the film "celebrates the beginning of the Age of Aquarius... the beginning of the pagan age and the end of Christianity." [Roger Ebert](https://www.rogerebert.com/interviews/interview-with-kenneth-anger)
**Connection [primary]:** *Invocation of My Demon Brother* (1969) — Moog score by Mick Jagger; features Anton LaVey, Bobby Beausoleil, and footage of the Rolling Stones (Hyde Park), Anita Pallenberg, Keith Richards, Marianne Faithfull; contains "flashes of the novel Moonchild (1917) written by... Aleister Crowley" [Sprüth Magers](https://spruethmagers.com/exhibitions/kenneth-anger-invocation-of-my-demon-brother-london/) (Sprüth Magers).
**Connection [primary/web-secondary]:** The Rolling Stones orbit — Anger lived with Jagger/Faithfull and later Richards/Pallenberg; Anger (Artnews): "It was a meeting of minds... They wanted to climb on the wavelength." Jagger scored *Invocation*; Faithfull appeared in *Lucifer Rising* as Lilith; Jimmy Page briefly attached to score *Lucifer Rising* before a falling-out.
**Connection [primary] — the Beausoleil bridge toward Manson:** Anger cast Bobby Beausoleil as Lucifer (met at the Invisible Circus, Glide Memorial Church, 1967). Beausoleil later murdered Gary Hinman on Manson's orders (1970) and recorded the *Lucifer Rising* soundtrack from Tracy Prison. Anger (Ebert): "One of the actors in my original version was Bobby Beausoleil, who got involved with the Manson family. I fired him from the film because he was dishonest... Two years later, he met Manson."
**Intent/causation edge:** That Anger's "teachings pushed" the Stones toward the occult is Anger's own framing plus web-secondary embellishment (e.g., the claim Anger's influence prompted "Sympathy for the Devil" — attributed, not documented). Beausoleil himself disputes parts of Anger's account (denied stealing the footage; denied a 1965 Hollywood meeting).
**Grade-exception:** Beausoleil's and Anger's accounts of the footage theft directly contradict; keep both open.

### Anchor 7 — → The Process Church of the Final Judgment
**Connection [primary/web-secondary]:** Robert Moor and Mary Ann MacLean met as Scientologists at the London org (Fitzroy Street, c. 1962–63), were declared Suppressive Persons/ejected, formed Compulsions Analysis (1963) using Scientology-derived "processing" and a "P-Scope" (based on the E-meter), which became the Process Church (London, 1966). Wikipedia: "Many of the group's therapeutic practices or 'processes'... and concepts were derived from Scientology, including the term 'processing.'" [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Process_Church_of_the_Final_Judgement)
**Connection [primary]:** Theology of four gods — Jehovah, Lucifer, Satan, Christ. Their own publications ("The Gods on War" 1967, "The Gods and Their People" 1970) lay out the system; Christ as unifying emissary, apocalyptic millennialism. [Oxford Academic](https://academic.oup.com/book/46787/chapter/413408013) Developed at Xtul, Yucatán.
**Grade-exception:** The Process explicitly did NOT practice magic (Wikipedia: "Unlike other Satanic groups... the Process Church did not practice magic") — so the "Crowley magick" throughline does NOT pass cleanly through the Process; the link to Crowley's rail is via Scientology's descent and shared "neo-Gnostic" structure, not ritual magick. Flag this as a break in the magical-lineage claim.

### Anchor 9 — The wider postwar occult-technical / Hollywood lattice
**Connection [web-secondary/primary]:** Manly P. Hall founded the Philosophical Research Society (PRS, 1934, Los Angeles), authored *The Secret Teachings of All Ages* (1928), and was a 33° Mason (recognized 1973). PRS was "a meeting place and a nexus for people in Los Angeles and on the West Coast who are interested in mythology, mysticism, esoteric subject matter." [Discover Los Angeles](https://www.discoverlosangeles.com/things-to-do/philosophical-research-society-the-story-of-an-la-icon)
**Connection to the milieu [web-secondary]:** Louis Sahagun's biography *Master of the Mysteries* recounts that Hall "crossed paths with everyone from Jack Parsons... to none other than Elvis Presley." [HuffPost](https://www.huffpost.com/entry/manly-palmer-hall-the-master-of-the-mysteries_b_57d04021e4b0eb9a57b6f245) This is the documented (if thin) contact point between Hall's orbit and Parsons's. **Connection [primary]:** The Hollywood/underground-film transmission is concrete — Cameron appeared in Anger's *Inauguration of the Pleasure Dome* (1954) and Curtis Harrington's *Wormwood Star* (1956) and *Night Tide* (1961, with Dennis Hopper); Craig Baldwin's *Mock Up on Mu* dramatizes the Parsons/Cameron/Hubbard story.
**Intent/causation edge:** That Hall "influenced" Parsons or the milieu is not documented beyond crossing paths; the throughline is a shared geographic-cultural lattice (mid-century LA), not a documented transmission of program.
**Grade-exception (thinnest node):** The Hall↔Parsons contact is the weakest documented link in the lattice — a single biographer's "crossed paths" anecdote, no primary correspondence located. Do not upgrade to influence.

## Recommendations
1. **Build the spine on the connection-edges only.** Every hop from Anchor 1 through Anchor 6 (Crowley→Agape→Parsons→Hubbard→Babalon→Anger) is primary-documentable and can be stated flatly, unhedged, with the quotes above. This is the load-bearing structure.
2. **Firewall every intent-edge behind its named claimant** and its grade. Use the verb to carry standing: "Parsons corresponded with Crowley" (happened) vs. "Atack argues Scientology derives from Crowley's magick" (argued, contested by Urban/Melton). Never let a documented visit (Process→Manson jail visit) leak into a documented direction.
3. **Treat the Process as a partial break in the magical lineage.** The Process descends from Scientology, not from ritual magick; flag that the "Crowley magick" throughline attenuates here (they "did not practice magic"). The continuity is structural/neo-Gnostic and organizational, not ritual.
4. **Catalogue the contested tail exhaustively but grade it low.** Sanders (retracted/settled), Terry (critically savaged — Horowitz, Baddeley), Dana (modern conspiracy synthesis) — all belong in the harvest as *claims that demonstrably exist in named work*, each at weak/apocryphal grade. High exception count = success.
5. **Benchmarks that would change grades:** (a) locating Parsons's/Hubbard's primary papers that show doctrinal transfer would upgrade the Scientology-from-Crowley edge from "argued" toward "documented"; (b) any primary Hall–Parsons correspondence would upgrade Anchor 9; (c) unsealed FBI files corroborating a Process operational role in any killing would upgrade the Terry edge — absent that, it stays apocryphal.

## Caveats
- **Contradictions kept open, not resolved:** lodge founding dates (1933 vs 1935); Parsons's motto (two Latin variants); Liber 49 reception date (Feb 27/28/29); the sum Hubbard took ($10K/$20K+/$21K). All recorded, none picked.
- **Hostile/unreliable witnesses flagged:** Ronald DeWolf (retracted other claims; hostile to his father); Scientology's LAPD-infiltration counter-narrative (self-serving, uncorroborated); OTO-succession web material (partisan).
- **Source-tier honesty:** Grokipedia and various Substack/enthusiast pages were used for orientation but the load-bearing quotes are from primary texts (Liber AL, *Book of Babalon*), named interviews (Anger in AnOther/Ebert/S8), court records (Justia/vLex), scholarship (Urban, UC Press *Nova Religio*), and institutional histories (OTO US Grand Lodge, Britannica).
- **The deliverable is a picture, not a proof.** The documented connections are dense and real; the intent that would make them a single directed project is exactly what is NOT documented. That gap is the finding.
