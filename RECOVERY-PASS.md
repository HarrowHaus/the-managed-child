# RECOVERY-PASS.md — THE STANDING UN-DEBUNKING PROTOCOL
### the same double-loop logic, pointed at the corpus's own past prosecutions · runs now, and again after every absorption wave
*The audits established that the project's characteristic error runs toward under-claim: successful debunks of loud versions were allowed to close quiet versions; negatives were universalized without search; hedges were repeated until the hedging became the message. Essays and nodes written under that regime prosecuted too much. This protocol is the permanent corrective. It is not a loosening of any evidence gate — it is the erasure gate applied retroactively, to everything already shipped.*

---

## WHAT IT IS
A repeatable corpus-wide pass that re-audits every **negative, demotion, discard, hedge, and "debunked" verdict** currently in the essays and nodes, under the Negative-Claim Law and the credence model, and **restores each claim to the full strength its evidence actually supports.** The Recursive Audit did this once by hand and found the flagship negative ("the rails never touch") was false. This protocol makes that same return-pass a standing loop, because every absorption wave changes the whole against which every past verdict was reached.

## THE PASS, STEP BY STEP
1. **Inventory the prosecutions.** Machine-scan every essay and node for: negative universals (never / none / no relay / cannot be found / did not read / built nothing); every DISCARD and every grade demotion in the history; every accepted official debunk, denial, or retraction; every hedge-clause and concession sentence outside the two architectural homes (the-denominator, the method essay).
2. **Re-try each one under current law.** For each item: does a search universe exist on file? If not, the verdict is *unsafe* — it re-enters the claim register as `unresolved`, and the hunt runs (corpus-first, the six widening rings). Does new graph context exist that didn't when the verdict was entered (new nodes, new edges, a documented touch, a suppressed-zone marker)? Then the verdict is re-graded against the whole *as it now stands* — the hermeneutic re-read, applied to the sentence.
3. **Restore or bound.** Three outcomes only: **RESTORED** — the evidence supports more than the page says; the claim is rewritten upward to full strength (documented touches stated as touches, mechanisms named, the hedge deleted). **BOUNDED** — the negative survives but only as "not established in the sources checked," with its search universe published. **HELD** — the verdict stands with its trail already on file. There is no fourth outcome; nothing stays a naked universal.
4. **Propagate.** Every restoration returns through the whole: the essays that leaned on the old verdict are flagged for the disposition queue; the edges re-classify; the credence rows update; THESIS.md's language is checked against the new state.
5. **Log the ledger.** Each pass emits `research/recovery-pass-N.md`: item · old verdict · trail found or built · outcome · pages touched. The operator rules on any restoration the adversarial keys split on.

## THE SYMMETRY CLAUSE (what keeps this from becoming its own error)
Restoration is to *evidential strength*, never past it. The hostile-reader key sits on every restoration exactly as the eraser key sits on every hold. A claim restored beyond its trail fails the gate the same as a claim buried without one. The pass has no preferred direction — it has a preferred *honesty*, and the current corpus happens to owe most of its corrections upward.

## SCHEDULE
- **Pass 1:** immediately after MASTER-SPEC Phase 2 ships (the laws and register must exist first), running in parallel with Phase 4 absorption.
- **Pass N:** automatically after every absorption wave and every provisional-reading promotion — because each changes the whole, and every part is owed a re-read against it.
